Business Plan — Digital Platform “Russian Service for Social Development Financing” (CPFSR)

Operator: Digital Platform CPFSR — a front-end / agent platform for the issuance of DFA backed by pledged assets. Stage 1 (pilot-light 2026–2028): placements through a technology partnership with an incumbent 779-P operator, funding from the VEB.RF Project Finance Factory (Government Decree No. 158) and the Federal Treasury. Stage 2 (2029+ horizon): expansion of the mandate to the model of targeted secured emission by the Bank of Russia — after a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months and adoption of the package of four federal-law amendments. Author of the concept and rightsholder of the know-how: Abdul-Khakim Akhmadovich Kagirov. Deposit certificate: No. 4011265 of 19.12.2024 (ireg.pro, GOST R 34.11-2012). Document date: 23.05.2026.

1. Project Executive Summary

What. CPFSR is a full-cycle front-end / agent platform for the issuance and servicing of digital financial assets (DFA) denominated against the pledge of real estate and other assets. Stage 1 (2026–2028): placements run through a technology partnership with an incumbent operator of the IS DFA under 779-P (Atomyze JSC or another register entity — the licence is non-transferable); the settlement channel and the funding — the VEB.RF Project Finance Factory (Government Decree No. 158 of 15.02.2018) and the Federal Treasury within the Budget Code. The Bank of Russia — the supervisory regulator under Article 4 of Federal Law 86-FZ; no additional issuance is envisaged in Stage 1. Stage 2 (2029+ horizon): a defensible path to the targeted secured-emission model of the Bank of Russia — implemented after a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months and adoption of the package of four federal-law amendments.

Why. Unlocking the “dormant” collateral base of the Russian Federation (conservatively RUB 25 tn) for targeted financing of national and infrastructure projects — without increasing the budget burden and with preservation of owners’ property rights. In Stage 1 the effect is achieved through infrastructure funding; in Stage 2 it is expanded through targeted secured emission.

For whom. - The state — an off-budget source of targeted funding for national projects. - Property owners (individuals and legal entities) — additional income while preserving the asset in their ownership. - Investors — a new class of reliable, secured and regulated instruments. - The financial sector (banks, insurers, appraisers) — new servicing revenues.

How much. - CapEx over 18 months — RUB 850M. - Total CapEx over 10 years — RUB 1,300M. - Peak financing need — RUB 1,302M (end of 2026).

When. - Start — 2025 (preparation, licences, R&D). - Launch of operations — 2026 (18 months from start). - Pilot — 2026–2027. - Scaling — 2028–2030. - Mature stage — from 2031.

How we earn. - DFA issuance commission — 0.5% of nominal. - Servicing commission — 0.3% of the outstanding stack. - Platform fee (valuation/insurance) — 0.15% of nominal. - B2B2G licences to partners — up to 0.15% of issuance volume. - Technology integrations — fixed.

What we ask. Starter funding of RUB 1.3 bn (CapEx + operating buffer for 18 months). Return — Payback 2.8 years (base scenario).

The recommended form is a joint-stock company (JSC): - It meets the CBR requirements for DFA operators (259-FZ, Article 10): minimum equity, corporate governance, requirements for the composition of shareholders. - It allows strategic investors to be attracted through share issuance. - It is compatible with the strategic state-asset regime (if such a model is approved).

3. Description of the product / service

3.1. What CPFSR is

CPFSR is a full-cycle digital platform that: 1. Accepts an application from the owner for a pledge of property (real estate, transport, intellectual property, securities, etc.). 2. Performs an automated appraisal of the collateral object with the involvement of accredited appraisers under Federal Law 135-FZ. 3. Records a temporary encumbrance on the object in the relevant state registers (Rosreestr, traffic police, depositories). 4. Coordinates the issuance parameters with the technology partner — the IS DFA operator under 779-P (the operator’s rules under Article 6 of Federal Law 259-FZ). 5. The technology partner issues the DFA at a nominal value corresponding to the collateral appraisal (subject to the discount standard). 6. Stage 1. Funding of the placement is provided through the VEB.RF Project Finance Factory (Government Decree No. 158 of 15.02.2018) and the Federal Treasury (Budget Code). Settlements — non-cash ruble or the digital ruble under 340-FZ as a settlement complement. 7. The funds are directed in a targeted manner to the financing of national and infrastructure projects in line with current federal laws and sub-legislative acts. 8. The DFA may be placed with professional securities-market participants; the proceeds from placement are credited to accounts under Articles 860.7–860.10 of the Civil Code. 9. Upon the completion of the project’s term, an exchange takes place: the DFA is redeemed, the encumbrance is lifted from the property, the owner recovers the object and receives the agreed financial remuneration.

Stage 2 (2029+ horizon). As part of the mandate expansion, steps 6–7 may be migrated to the targeted secured-emission model of the Bank of Russia against a pool of pledged DFA, with the escrow account managed by the CBR. Stage 2 is implemented after a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months and adoption of the package of four federal-law amendments (see Legal Framework § 8.2).

4. Market analysis

4.1. DFA market in the RF

Since 2022 Federal Law 259-FZ “On Digital Financial Assets” has been in force in the Russian Federation, establishing the legal framework for the issuance and circulation of DFA. Operators of information systems accredited by the Bank of Russia are operating on the market (the information is published in the CBR register).

Based on open data, the cumulative DFA issuance volume in the RF for 2023–2025 is measured in hundreds of billions of rubles and shows confident growth. The dominant classes are monetary claims, corporate DFA, DFA on metals. DFA backed by real-estate collateral with direct CBR issuance are absent from current market practice.

4.3. Demand segmentation

Individuals (B2C): - Owners of residential real estate with stable cash flow, looking for ways to monetise the asset without selling. - Target group — around 30–40% of households in large and medium-sized cities of the RF.

Legal entities (B2B): - Owners of commercial real estate — landlords, developers, retail chains. - Industrial enterprises with land assets. - Holdings with non-core assets.

State enterprises (B2G): - Owners of infrastructure and socially significant assets (including through subsidiaries). - Regions and municipalities — for financing regional programmes via the pledge of municipal property.

4.4. Competitors

In Stage 1 (wedge-pilot 2026–2028) the existing 779-P register operators (Sber CFA, Alfa CFA, Atomyze JSC, Masterchain) are potential technology partners, not competitors at the licensing level. Indirect competitors: bank lending against real-estate collateral (slower, more expensive, with no targeted focus on national projects); crowdfunding and investment platforms (smaller, without the state perimeter).

CPFSR’s competitive advantage is the two-stage architecture: in Stage 1 — a specialised front-end layer over a 779-P operator linked to infrastructure funding through the VEB.RF Factory (Government Decree No. 158) + the Federal Treasury (Budget Code) and targeted placement in national projects, with property preserved in the owner’s hands. In Stage 2 (2029+ horizon) — a defensible path to the targeted secured-emission model of the Bank of Russia. This is not the “best” product in the existing category, but a new category of targeted infrastructure DFA.

4.5. Regulatory environment

Current base: - 86-FZ “On the Central Bank of the Russian Federation (Bank of Russia)” - 259-FZ “On Digital Financial Assets, the Digital Currency and on Amendments to Certain Legislative Acts of the Russian Federation” - 39-FZ “On the Securities Market” - 102-FZ “On Mortgage (Pledge of Real Estate)” - 218-FZ “On State Real Estate Registration” - Tax Code (Chapters 21, 25, 34)

Stage 1 (2026–2028): regulatory changes are not required. The near contour is launched on the current base of 259-FZ + 779-P + Budget Code + Government Decree No. 158 + Civil Code. Adjustments are made at the level of the operator’s rules under Article 6 of Federal Law 259-FZ (approved by the Bank of Russia) and the contract structure with the technology partner (Atomyze or another 779-P register entity).

Stage 2 (2029+ horizon): the package of four federal-law amendments. Entry condition — a positive Stage 1 track record of at least 12 months of industrial operation and an inter-agency position of the Ministry of Economic Development / Ministry of Finance / VEB.RF / State Duma. Composition: - 86-FZ — formalisation of the institute of targeted secured emission against a pool of pledged DFA. - 259-FZ — the category of “targeted infrastructure DFA” and regulation of the exchange procedure. - Budget Code — procedure for the Federal Treasury to receive the proceeds of the targeted emission. - Civil Code — extension of the escrow institute to special accounts of the Bank of Russia for the placement of the targeted-emission proceeds. - Sub-legislative acts: government decrees, a CBR Directive, a joint order of the Ministry of Finance / CBR / Ministry of Digital Development / Rosreestr / Federal Treasury.

This block is described in a separate “Legal Framework” project document.

Unit economics are positive from the first issuance. The high LTV/CAC ratio is driven by servicing duration (5-year average DFA life) and automation of valuation processes.

5.3. Partnership model

Banks. CPFSR integrates into banking apps via API. A partner bank acts as the acquisition channel and receives a revenue share (e.g., 30–50% of the issuance commission). This delivers a new service line to the bank without additional regulatory requirements.

Insurance companies. Mandatory insurance of the pledged property — a built-in platform module. The insurer earns the premium + cross-sales (motor, household). The platform takes an agency fee.

Appraisal firms. Accredited appraisers conduct the initial collateral valuation; the platform automates 70–80% of the process (mass valuation + desk review). The appraiser receives a fixed fee.

Technology integrators. Embedding the platform into a partner’s infrastructure — a separate service paid on a fixed-project basis.

7. Technology plan

The CPFSR technology contour is described in a separate document “Technical documentation with references to federal laws and CBR instructions” (folder 01_Техническая_документация).

Key architectural principles: 1. Microservices architecture on a stack of Russian technologies (Astra Linux, DBMS from the software register, Russian cryptographic modules). 2. Geo-replication between two data centres with RPO ≤ 5 min, RTO ≤ 30 min. 3. A multi-layer security model (compliance with 152-FZ, FSTEC requirements, GOSTs). 4. An integration bus to connect banks, insurers, appraisers and state authorities. 5. Digital identity — Gosuslugi, SBP (for individuals); e-signature and EDI — for legal entities. 6. A distributed register for DFA recording (on a certified Russian platform). 7. Full compliance with the CBR requirements for DFA operators (regulations, instructions, standards).

Client-side readiness (as of 2025)

By the moment of negotiations with the Bank of Russia and investors, the client side of the platform is fully developed:

  • Progressive Web App mobile application — 23 screens, the full 19-step life cycle of the author (from registration to DFA exchange), 4 themes, 4 languages (Russian, English, French, Arabic with RTL), Service Worker for offline mode, manifest.json for device installation.
  • GOST 19 design documentation — 12 documents from the Technical Assignment (19.201) to the Schedule of Operational Documents (19.507), ready for submission to the Ministry of Digital Development register (Government Decree No. 1236) and for FSTEC / FSB certification.
  • Capacitor 6 platform builds — configuration and instructions for packaging into native Android (AAB for RuStore and Google Play), iOS (App Store), Windows (MSIX for Microsoft Store) applications.
  • RuStore publication pack — a catalogue description, a privacy policy (under 152-FZ), terms of use, promo materials, a publication checklist.

The readiness of the client side cuts the time-to-pilot by approximately 3 months and reduces the technology risk of development. This is reflected in the updated international valuation of the project (Reasoned Value pre-money — USD 80M in the base zone).

Specific names are not provided: team selection is a starter-stage task, undertaken in parallel with attracting a strategic investor. All appointments are subject to regulator approval (the CBR sets reputation and qualification requirements for managers of operators).

11.3. Know-how rightsholder protection

After contribution of the author’s know-how to the charter capital of the operator JSC, the following protective mechanisms are fixed (in detail — _Мастер/Концепт.md § 1-quater):

  1. A permanent seat for author A.A.A. Kagirov on the Board of Directors until Series B (≥ 2031), independent of the size of the investor’s stake.
  2. Protective covenants in the Shareholders Agreement: drag-along — only with the rightsholder’s participation; tag-along — 100% protection from a forced exit; anti-dilution full ratchet until Series B; prohibition on assigning know-how rights without the rightsholder’s consent; vesting of the institutional shareholder of 4 years with a 1-year cliff.
  3. Reverter of the know-how upon liquidation / bankruptcy / hostile takeover: the exclusive rights revert to the author unconditionally.
  4. A 2% royalty on the operator’s revenue payable to the rightsholder as licensor (alongside share dividends).

11.4. EBITDA margin: bridge 2030 → 2034

Wedge_pilot-light.md § 5.2 provides the EBITDA margin trajectory: 2027 = 20%, 2028 = 40%, 2029 = 55%, 2030 = 65%. The TV target margin in 2034 = 74.8%. The 65% → 74.8% gap (+9.8 p.p. over 4 years) is explained by three factors: (a) the outstanding DFA stack by 2034 ≈ RUB 26.6 tn yields a high share of servicing commission (0.3% × low marginal OpEx); (b) automation of compliance/KYC through integrations with ESIA/UBS/GIS GMP → reduction of incremental cost-to-serve to ~3% of 2034 revenue vs 8% in 2030; (c) OpEx add-ons for appraisers/insurers and legal support become fixed by volume (≈ 5% of revenue) once a mature partner pool is reached. Details — _Мастер/Единые_показатели_v2.md § 4 (FCF series) and the “EBITDA bridge 2030-2034” sheet in _shared/finmodel.js.

12. Socio-economic effect

12.1. Impact on GDP

The financing volume attracted via CPFSR in the base scenario: - 2026: RUB 130 bn of issuance → ~RUB 130 bn of infrastructure funding through the VEB.RF Factory + Federal Treasury (Stage 1) → reinvested into national projects. - 2029: RUB 2.9 tn annual issuance. On the Stage 2 horizon (after adoption of the package of four federal-law amendments) — transition to the targeted secured-emission model of the Bank of Russia. - 2034: RUB 7.1 tn annual issuance.

At an infrastructure investment multiplier of 1.8–2.3 (the standard range for the Russian economy; the Ministry of Economic Development uses 1.9–2.1 for assessing national-project effects), the expected GDP contribution from CPFSR investments: - 2029: RUB 5.2–6.7 tn of additional economic activity. - 2034: RUB 12.8–16.4 tn of additional economic activity.

This is comparable to 5–8% of the nominal GDP of the RF (for 2034).

12.2. Impact on national projects

CPFSR provides targeted financing of national projects without increasing the budget burden. Reduction of the financing queue through mobilisation of the private collateral base is the key effect, directly stated in the 6th advantage of the author’s concept.

Potential target areas (defined by sectoral federal laws and government decrees): - Heavy industry and machine-building. - Import substitution in critical sectors. - Healthcare (infrastructure modernisation, equipment). - Science and education. - Transport infrastructure (bridges, roads, logistics hubs). - Energy and utilities.

12.3. Impact on “dormant” assets

Per the 4th advantage of the concept — bringing “dormant” private assets into the economic turnover.

At present, around 80% of residential and a significant share of commercial real estate in the RF are not used as collateral instruments for objective reasons (paperwork, procedural duration, no suitable product). CPFSR removes these barriers via automation and a new product contour. By Year 10 — up to 20% of the conservative collateral capacity is in circulation (RUB 5 tn), corresponding to the mobilisation of assets of 10–20 million households and enterprises in the RF.

12.4. Additional income for owners

Per the 9th point of the concept — financial remuneration for the owner of the pledged property.

Under an owner-remuneration model of, for example, 1–2% p.a. of the DFA nominal (the exact rate is set by regulatory standards and the contract with the operator), aggregate payouts to owners by Year 10: - DFA stack volume: RUB 26.6 tn. - At 1% — RUB 266 bn in additional owner income annually. - At 2% — RUB 532 bn annually.

This is comparable to the budget of several RF regions and provides a significant “secondary” contribution to consumer demand and the tax base.

12.5. Impact on the financial system

  • Expansion of the securities-market instrument base (a new class of reliable assets).
  • Diversification opportunities for pension and insurance portfolios.
  • Development of adjacent services (valuation, insurance, legal support) — additional jobs in the financial sector.
  • Impetus to the development of Russian fintech and regulatory technology.

12.6. Inflationary regime

Per the 10th point of the concept — issuance is against fully secured assets. This is fundamentally different from “helicopter” emission: every ruble issued is backed by real, appraised-at-discount property. The inflationary effect is neutral or disinflationary (growth in goods and services supply through national projects offsets the increase in the money supply).
