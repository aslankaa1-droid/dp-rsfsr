RFP — Legal Opinion

Автор: Кагиров А.-Х.А. · Свидетельство о депонировании №4011265 от 19.12.2024 · Center Group Company · Engagement: CPFSR-RF-DESIGN-2026

RFP — Legal Opinion from a tier-1 firm

Project: Digital Platform CPFSR — operator of the DFA information system backed by real-estate collateral, with DFA issuance for national projects Client: Abdul-Khakim Akhmadovich Kagirov (Center Group Company) — sole rightsholder of the concept (deposit certificate No. 4011265 of 19.12.2024) Engagement reference: RSFSR-LEGAL-2026 RFP issue date: 24.05.2026 Bid deadline: T+14 days (08.06.2026)

1. Engagement objective

Obtaining an independent tier-1 legal opinion on the project in order to: - Lift the regulatory risk flag for tier-1 institutional investors and Limited Partners; - Confirm the legal defensibility of the wedge construction (Stage 1 wedge-pilot 2026–2028 without amendments to 86-FZ vs Stage 2 horizon 2029+ with the package of four federal laws); - Obtain a formal sign-off on the readiness of the escrow scheme and the contract pack for a commercial launch.

2. Scope of legal review

2.1. DFA regulatory regime

  • Bank of Russia Regulation No. 779-P — regime for inclusion of an operator in the IS DFA operators register; soft-launch path in Stage 1.
  • Bank of Russia Directive No. 4336-U — DFA accounting methodology; mandatory reporting.
  • Federal Law 86-FZ Article 4 — the Bank of Russia mandate; boundaries of Stage 1 / Stage 2.
  • Federal Law 259-FZ “On DFA” — operational foundation.
  • Federal Law 39-FZ — boundary between DFA and securities for qualification purposes.

Survey points: is it legal to structure the pilot-light through Atomyze JSC as the 779-P operator (sublease vs separate operator); is a separate register status required for the CPFSR front-end; deployment-ready configuration limits prior to formal amendments.

2.2. Escrow construction

  • Civil Code Articles 860.7–860.10 — escrow account at the banking level.
  • Federal Law 161-FZ — the National Payment System.
  • Structure of the escrow agent: bank vs specialised company vs VEB.RF.
  • Triggers for fund transfer: collateral verified / DFA issued / national project reported.
  • Disclosure to the end investor (natural person).

2.3. DFA tax position

  • Tax Code sub-para 12.2 of para 2 of Art. 149 — VAT exemption of DFA transactions (introduced by Federal Law 324-FZ of 31.07.2020).
  • Tax Code Art. 34.2 — specifics of taxation of digital rights.
  • Tax Code Arts. 309–310 — tax on income of foreign legal entities from DFA.
  • IT preferential rate of 5% (until 31.12.2030) with ≥ 70% IT revenue.

A written legal opinion is required: does sub-para 12.2 of Art. 149 apply to commission income of the issuing operator, or only to transactions in the DFA themselves.

2.4. Collateral regime

  • Civil Code § 3 of Chapter 23 (Arts. 334–358.18) — mortgage and pledge of real estate.
  • Federal Law 102-FZ “On Mortgage” — registration of the mortgage in the State Real Estate Register.
  • Federal Law 218-FZ “On State Real Estate Registration” — note of a digital pledge in the State Real Estate Register.
  • Civil Code Art. 358.6 — pledge of rights of claim (relevant for tokenised collateral).

Survey point: is the “real-estate pledge → DFA” mechanic compatible with the existing 102-FZ regime without the need for amendments?

2.5. Corporate protection of the author (SHA construction)

  • The author’s stake (A.-Kh. A. Kagirov) — protected until Series B through a preferred class + drag-along restrictions.
  • Reverse vesting on the know-how (Article 1465 CC) and on the software (Article 1262 CC) upon founder exit.
  • 2% royalty on the operator’s revenue payable to the concept rightsholder (post Series B); cap-table mechanics.
  • Anti-dilution full ratchet until Series B.
  • Drag-along, tag-along, RoFR — standard condition precedents.

2.6. KYC / AML / CFT

  • Federal Law 115-FZ — counteraction of legalisation of criminal proceeds.
  • CBR Regulation No. 375-P — KYC procedures for financial organisations.
  • CBR Directive No. 5599-U — simplified identification via the Unified Biometric System / ESIA.
  • FATF Recommendations — international AML standards (for friendly jurisdictions).
  • Travel Rule for DFA (by analogy with FATF Recommendation 16 for VASP).

2.7. EULA / customer protection

  • Federal Law “On Protection of Consumer Rights” Arts. 13, 15, 16 — liability limits, Art. 16 nullity of limitations.
  • Civil Code Arts. 393, 1064, 1095 — liability for harm.
  • Federal Law 152-FZ “On Personal Data” — processing of customer personal data.
  • GDPR (EU Regulation 2016/679) — where applicable for non-residents.

2.8. IP regime (know-how protection)

  • Civil Code Article 1465 — trade-secret regime.
  • Civil Code Article 1262 — software.
  • Deposit certificate No. 4011265 of 19.12.2024 (ireg.pro, GOST R 34.11-2012).
  • Protection against disclosure in the DD process through NDA + ring-fencing.

3. Deliverables

Document Format Deadline
Inception Memo (kick-off scope) PDF, 5–8 pp T+7 days
Preliminary Findings PDF, 12–20 pp T+14 days
Final Legal Opinion (signed by Partner) PDF, 30–60 pp, with Partner’s signature T+28 days
Q&A management sessions 2 × 1.5 h By schedule
Executive summary for the investor memorandum PDF, 4–6 pp T+28 days

All deliverables — in Russian (primary). The executive summary also in English.

4. Quality and independence

  • The signing Partner must be a registered Russian Bar member + admitted to work on financial matters (experience in DFA / banking law / financial regulation ≥ 5 years).
  • Team — minimum 3 persons: Partner + Senior Associate + Junior. CVs mandatory.
  • Conflict-of-interest disclosure: must not have acted against any party to the project (Atomyze JSC, VEB.RF, Ministry of Economic Development, Ministry of Finance, Bank of Russia) over the last 24 months.
  • Insurance: PI cover ≥ USD 3M.
