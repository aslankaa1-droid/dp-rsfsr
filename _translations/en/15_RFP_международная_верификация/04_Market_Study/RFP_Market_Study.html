RFP — Market Study

Автор: Кагиров А.-Х.А. · Свидетельство о депонировании №4011265 от 19.12.2024 · Center Group Company · Engagement: CPFSR-RF-DESIGN-2026

RFP — Market Study from a tier-1 strategy firm

Project: CPFSR — DFA operator backed by real-estate collateral with DFA issuance for national projects Client: A.-Kh. A. Kagirov (Center Group Company) — sole rightsholder (deposit certificate No. 4011265 of 19.12.2024) Engagement reference: RSFSR-MARKET-2026 Issue date: 24.05.2026 Bid deadline: T+21 days (15.06.2026)

1. Engagement objective

Obtaining an independent market study from a tier-1 strategy firm, in order to: - Triangulate the TAM / SAM / SOM of the DFA market in the Russian Federation + EAEU + friendly jurisdictions on the 2026–2034 horizon. - Validate the market positioning of CPFSR against competitors (Atomyze JSC, SPB Exchange, Sber CFA, Alfa-CFA). - Confirm the unit economics on reference transactions (commission structure, AUM, churn rates). - Create an authoritative reference for the investor memorandum.

2. Scope of Work

2.1. Market sizing

  • TAM: Total real-estate collateral capacity in the Russian Federation (the current concept figure of RUB 25 tn requires independent validation).
  • SAM: The addressable share for DFA tokenisation (taking into account regulatory limits of 779-P + 259-FZ).
  • SOM: Achievable share by 2034 (concept: 18% Base).
  • Growth drivers:
    • Adoption of the package of four federal laws (Stage 2 horizon)
    • Activation of the VEB.RF Project Finance Factory (Government Decree No. 158)
    • Expansion of the Bank of Russia mandate over the regulation of DFA (Article 4 of 86-FZ)
    • Tax preferences (sub-para 12.2 of Art. 149 of the Tax Code; IT preferential rate of 5%)
    • Escalation of national projects (12 national projects with a budget of RUB 13.2 tn for 2026–2030)
  • Segmentation by asset class:
    • Residential stock (4.7 bn m² × RUB 85,000/m² = RUB 400 tn market)
    • Commercial real estate (RUB 45 tn)
    • Land plots (RUB 62 tn)
    • Movable assets, transport, IP (Stage 2 horizon)
  • Data sources: Rosstat, the Bank of Russia, ANCOB (Association of Digital Assets), Aton IC, Refinitiv, PitchBook, CB Insights, OECD, IMF.

2.2. Competitive landscape

  • Atomyze JSC — incumbent 779-P operator, technology partner in the wedge construction (LoI required per Pre-meeting_briefs/01).
  • Sber CFA — major player with a banking balance sheet.
  • Alfa-Bank CFA — focus on corporate DFA.
  • SPB Exchange CFA — focus on retail investors.
  • Toko / other operators — niche players.
  • International peers (for USD-WACC comparison):
    • Securitize, Polymath (general-purpose tokenisation)
    • HQLAx (collateral management)
    • Bakkt (regulated crypto custody)
    • Polymath (security tokens)

Analysis: positioning of CPFSR as a unique wedge at the intersection of (1) real-estate collateral + (2) financing of national projects + (3) DFA wrapper. Assessment of barriers to entry and defensibility.

2.3. Unit economics on reference transactions

  • AUM commissions for tier-1 Russian fintech operators: 0.15% — 0.30% p.a.
  • Issuance fees: 0.5% — 1.5% of the issued volume.
  • Service fees: 0.3% — 0.8% p.a. on the stack size.
  • B2B fees: bespoke.
  • Operational margin of mature tier-1 platforms: 60% — 75% (EBITDA).
  • Validation of sensitivity to market share / commissions / OpEx curve.

2.4. Comparable transactions / valuation benchmarks

A minimum of 15 comparable transactions from 2023–2026 in the segments: - DFA operators of the Russian Federation (private + public) - Russian financial marketplaces - International tokenisation platforms (Securitize, Polymath, HQLAx, Tokeny, Anchorage) - Cryptocurrency exchanges under regulation (Coinbase, Kraken)

Multiples: - EV / Revenue (current + forward) - EV / EBITDA (current + forward) - EV / AUM (for assets under management) - EV / Issuance volume (for the issuance model)

2.5. Regulatory outlook

  • Analysis of acts of the Bank of Russia, Ministry of Finance and Ministry of Economic Development over 2024–2026.
  • Forecasts on adoption of amendments to 86-FZ and 259-FZ (Stage 2 horizon).
  • Comparative analysis with the US (SEC + CFTC), the EU (MiCA), the UK (FCA), Singapore (MAS), and the UAE (VARA).
  • Implications for CPFSR in each jurisdiction.

  • Source quality: Tier 1 (PitchBook, Crunchbase, Refinitiv, S&P Capital IQ, Bloomberg) + government statistics + primary interviews.
