Integrations with state information systems

The document describes the integrations of CPFSR with the state information systems of the Russian Federation in compliance with the requirements of FZ-149, SMEV 3, FZ-152, FZ-187 and the regulations of the owning agencies.

2. Detailed regulations of key integrations

2.1. ESIA (Gosuslugi)

  • Standard: Methodological recommendations on the application of ESIA (Ministry of Digital Development).
  • Authentication: OpenID Connect 1.0.
  • Account level: Confirmed (UZ-3).
  • Scenarios: registration, authorisation, receipt of personal data subject to user consent, inter-agency check.
  • Specifics: connection through a secured channel is required; user consent is given on the ESIA page.

2.2. EBS (Unified Biometric System)

  • Standard: Bank of Russia Regulation No. 482-P.
  • Purpose: biometric identification for the remote submission of assets and signing of contracts.
  • Biometric type: voice + face (multimodal).
  • Specifics: the platform acts as a consumer of EBS; transmission of biometric vectors is prohibited, only verification by identifier.

2.3. EGRN (Rosreestr)

  • Standard: Regulation on electronic interaction with Rosreestr.
  • Scenarios:
    • receipt of information on a real estate object by cadastral number (for valuation);
    • filing of an application for state registration of an encumbrance (pledge);
    • filing of an application for the removal of the encumbrance after exchange / expiry of the term.
  • Specifics: all requests are signed with the platform operator’s UKEP.

2.4. Bank of Russia — register of IS operators

  • Regulation: Regulation No. 779-P.
  • Regular reports:
    • volume of issued DFAs (monthly);
    • register of active DFAs (quarterly);
    • operator’s financial indicators (quarterly);
    • IS incidents (within 24 hours of detection).

2.5. Bank of Russia — digital rouble platform

  • Regulation: Chapter X of FZ-86 (after No. 340-FZ).
  • Stage 1 scenarios (pilot-light 2026–2028): the digital rouble is a settlement complement (one of the options of cashless payment for the issue of DFAs), without an emission link from the Bank of Russia.
  • Stage 2 scenarios (horizon of 2029+, after the adoption of the package of 4 federal-law amendments):
    • request for the issue of digital roubles against the collateral DFA pool;
    • receipt of confirmation of credit to a special digital wallet;
    • initiation of a transfer to the Federal Treasury’s digital wallet.

2.6. Federal Treasury

  • Purpose: at Stage 1 — receipt of funds of infrastructure funding through the VEB.RF Factory (RF Government Resolution No. 158) and the Budget Code; confirmation of targeted expenditure; report on the implementation of the project. At Stage 2 (horizon of 2029+) — receipt of funds of the Bank of Russia’s targeted secured emission within the framework of amendments to the Budget Code of the RF.
  • Protocol: SMEV 3 + secured reports in the standardised forms of the FK.
  • Notifications: at each stage — automatic notifications in the DLT register and the audit log.

2.7. Rosfinmonitoring

  • Regulation: FZ-115, Rosfinmonitoring Order No. 366.
  • Reporting:
    • mandatory operations control;
    • suspicious operations (under a risk-based approach);
    • information on beneficial owners.
  • Specifics: the platform sends reports in the established XML formats of the Federal Electronic Service.

3. Security of integrations

  • All channels are encrypted using GOST TLS / ViPNet.
  • All requests — with mutual mTLS authentication.
  • All outgoing transactions are signed by the operator’s UKEP.
  • All requests are logged in the immutable audit log with tracing.
  • All integration gateways are placed in the DMZ segment with a dedicated WAF.

5. Evolution of integrations

  • Phase 1 (2025–2026): the basic set INT-01–INT-11.
  • Phase 2 (2026–2027): addition of INT-12–INT-17 and extended digital rouble scenarios.
  • Phase 3 (2027+): inter-state integrations (BRICS+, EAEU).

6. Management of integration incidents

  • 24×7 availability monitoring of each integration.
  • Alerts on threshold exceedance (latency, error rate).
  • Regular reviews with the teams of the owning agencies.
  • Backup channels for critical integrations (Bank of Russia, Federal Treasury).
