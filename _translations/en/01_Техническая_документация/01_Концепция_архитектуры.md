## 1. Purpose and scope of application

The CPFSR platform is a state-significant operator information system for the issue of digital financial assets (DFA) collateralised by the property of citizens and organisations of the Russian Federation. In the pilot-light regime of 2026–2028, the targeted funding of national and infrastructure projects against the collateral of a DFA pool is organised through the infrastructure circuit of VEB.RF (the Project Finance Factory, Government Resolution No. 158 of 15.02.2018) and the Federal Treasury within the framework of the current Budget Code of the Russian Federation. The Bank of Russia acts as the general supervisory regulator of the financial market under Art. 4 of Federal Law No. 86-FZ. On the horizon of 2029+, a transition to a model of targeted secured emission by the Bank of Russia is possible (with the use of the digital rouble) upon the adoption of the corresponding package of regulatory acts and a positive track record of the pilot-light.

The document describes:
- general architectural principles of the platform;
- logical and physical architecture;
- the technology stack on fully import-substituted components;
- the reliability and fault-tolerance model;
- requirements for the classification of the information system as an object of critical information infrastructure (CII).

## 3. Logical architecture — 7 layers

1. **Asset owner channel** — web portal, iOS/Android/Windows mobile applications, chatbot in state channels.
2. **Backend-for-Frontend (BFF)** — an optimised aggregation layer for each channel.
3. **API Gateway** — a single entry point, authentication, rate-limiting, antifraud filters.
4. **Business services** — microservices: KYC, valuation, insurance, DFA issue, smart contracts, register, escrow adapter, reporting, notifications.
5. **Data layer** — relational DBMS (Postgres Pro / Red DB), document stores, in-memory cache (a domestic Redis analogue), S3-compatible object storage.
6. **Integration layer** — adapters to state information systems (Gosuslugi, EGRN, SMEV 3, GIS GMP, FK, the Central Bank, Rosfinmonitoring, FNS, NSPK).
7. **Security layer** — DLP, SIEM, EDR, GosSOPKA-agent, HSM modules (KriptoPro HSM), monitoring and audit systems.

## 4. Physical architecture

### 4.1. Geography
- A minimum of 3 active data centres on the territory of the Russian Federation in different federal districts (Central, Siberian, Ural — an example).
- An active-active-active topology with synchronous replication for critical circuits.
- A reserve "cold" data centre for DR scenarios.

### 4.2. Network model
- Two-tier perimeter protection: tier 1 — the state network "RTKomm", tier 2 — own perimeter with domestic NGFW.
- An isolated segment for integrations with the Bank of Russia (SPFS, the secured ViPNet channel).
- Full segmentation by microservices (zero-trust network).

### 4.3. Execution platform
- Containerisation on domestic Kubernetes distributions (Deckhouse Stronghold / Yandex Managed K8s on-prem analogues).
- Service Mesh with KriptoPro certificates for mTLS.

## 5. Technology stack

### 5.1. System software
- Operating systems: Astra Linux Special Edition, Red OS, Alt Server — FSTEC-certified.
- Hypervisor: zVirt / SberCloud / vStack (domestic VMware analogues).
- Containerisation: domestic Kubernetes distributions.

### 5.2. Databases and storage
- Relational: Postgres Pro Enterprise, Tantor Special Edition, Red DB.
- Analytical: Pangolin, Arenadata DB.
- Document-oriented: Postgres Pro Multimaster JSONB; or a domestic MongoDB analogue (Atlasync).
- Object: Yandex Object Storage on-prem, Arkuda, S3-compatible host.

### 5.3. Brokers and stream processing
- Apache Kafka in the distributions Arenadata Streaming / Yandex Managed Service for Apache Kafka.
- RabbitMQ as a fallback for classical queues.

### 5.4. DLT layer (distributed DFA register)
- Based on a domestic platform of the "Masterchain" / Waves Enterprise class; the specific vendor is agreed with the Bank of Russia at the pilot stage.
- Hashing of operations — GOST R 34.11-2012.
- Electronic signature of register operations — GOST R 34.10-2012.

### 5.5. Cryptography
- HSM: KriptoPro HSM / ViPNet HSM.
- SDK: KriptoPro CSP 5.0, ViPNet CSP.
- Certificates: Qualified certificates of certification authorities accredited by the Ministry of Digital Development.

### 5.6. Information security
- SIEM: KOMRAD, MaxPatrol SIEM, R-Vision SIEM.
- EDR: Kaspersky EDR / R-Vision EDR.
- DLP: Solar Dozor / InfoWatch.
- WAF: PT Application Firewall / SolidWall.
- Code analysis systems: PT Application Inspector, AppSec.Hub.
- VPN: ViPNet, KontinentAP.

### 5.7. Observability
- Metrics: VictoriaMetrics, a domestic Prometheus stack.
- Logs: Loki / a domestic analogue.
- Tracing: Jaeger / a domestic OpenTelemetry stack.
- Dashboards: Grafana OSS on own infrastructure.

## 6. Architectural principles

1. **State significance is paramount.** Security, reliability and regulatory compatibility take priority over development speed.
2. **100% import independence.** All components — from the Ministry of Digital Development register or certified by FSTEC.
3. **Event-driven.** All business processes (DFA issue, exchange, insurance event) are asynchronous events with traceability.
4. **Immutable audit log.** All key operations are written to the DLT register and to a centralised audit log; the data are immutable.
5. **Zero-Trust.** Every call between microservices is performed with mTLS authentication.
6. **Secure by Design.** Security is built in from the architecture stage (Privacy by Design, KIIS by Design).
7. **Cloud-agnostic on-prem.** The platform is deployed on its own infrastructure without dependence on foreign clouds.
8. **API-first.** All integrations are through documented APIs (OpenAPI 3.1).
9. **GitOps + IaC.** Infrastructure and configurations are in git with automatic deployment (Argo CD / a domestic analogue).
10. **N+2 redundancy level.** Critical services have double redundancy.
