# Security and CII of CPFSR

The document describes the threat model, the information protection system, and the operating regime of the platform as an object of critical information infrastructure (FZ-187).

---

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|

| Personal data security level (RF Government Resolution No. 1119) | UZ-1 |
| Protection level under GOST R 57580.1 | 4 (maximum) |
| State IS class (FSTEC Order No. 17) | K1 |
| Type of information security tools | FSTEC / FSB-certified |

## 2. Threat model

The threat model is built in line with the FSTEC of Russia threat database (BDU FSTEC) and the FSTEC Methodological Document "Methodology for the assessment of information security threats" (2021).

### Key threat groups

1. **External intruders (high motivation):** APT groups targeting the financial and state infrastructure of the RF. Targets: theft of funds, disruption of the platform, compromise of the issue circuit.
2. **Internal intruders:** unfair employees of the operator, contractors, partners.
3. **Social engineering:** phishing of the operator's employees and of platform users.
4. **Supply chain attacks:** compromise of information security tools, libraries, updates.
5. **DDoS attacks:** on public interfaces and APIs.
6. **Cryptography attacks:** attempts to compromise keys, intercept sessions.
7. **Physical threats:** intrusion into the data centre, theft of media.
8. **Availability threats:** hardware failures, operating errors.
9. **Data integrity threats:** modification of records in DBs and registers.
10. **Confidentiality threats:** leaks of personal data and financial information.

### Damage levels
- Level I (catastrophic): compromise of the issue circuit; unacceptable, requires maximum protection.
- Level II (severe): mass leak of personal data; unacceptable.
- Level III (moderate): failure of a single component; recovered within RTO.

## 3. Information protection system

### 3.1. Perimeter protection
- Domestic NGFW (UserGate / SolarSpace).
- WAF (PT Application Firewall).
- Anti-DDoS (Qrator Labs Russia / Stormwall).
- Anti-bot protection.
- Network segmentation under the zero-trust principles.

### 3.2. Protection of administrator workstations
- EDR (Kaspersky EDR Expert / R-Vision EDR).
- DLP (Solar Dozor / InfoWatch).
- Removable media control (prohibited by default).
- Two-factor authentication for all administrator accesses.

### 3.3. Data protection
- Encryption of data at rest (KriptoPro Disk).
- Encryption of data in transit (GOST TLS, ViPNet).
- Masking of personal data in non-production environments.
- Tokenisation of sensitive fields (DB).
- Backup on encrypted media.

### 3.4. Application protection
- Secure development (Secure SDLC):
  - SAST on every commit;
  - DAST weekly;
  - SCA (software composition analysis);
  - Pentest per release.
- Signing of releases with UKEP.
- Isolated runtime environments (gVisor / Kata Containers).

### 3.5. Access management
- Principle of least privilege.
- RBAC + ABAC at the service level.
- Two-factor authentication for all employees.
- Privileged Access Management (PAM) for administrators.
- Journalising of all privileged operations.

### 3.6. Cryptographic components
- KriptoPro / ViPNet HSM — for storage of root keys.
- Regular key rotation (at least once every 12 months for working keys, every 5 years for root keys).
- Hardware UKEP tokens for employees.

## 4. Compliance with regulatory requirements

### 4.1. FZ-187 "On the Security of CII"
- The categorisation of the CII object has been carried out in the established manner.
- Notification has been sent to FSTEC.
- Connection to GosSOPKA via a secured channel.
- An officer responsible for the security of the significant CII objects has been appointed.
- Continuous interaction with NCCCI.

### 4.2. Bank of Russia Instruction No. 6635-U
- Regulation of IS incident handling.
- Notification to the Bank of Russia within the established timeframes (24 h / 72 h / 30 days depending on the category).
- Incident journal with retention of at least 5 years.

### 4.3. FSTEC Order No. 239
- Application of information security tools for significant CII objects of the 1st category.
- Regular compliance checks.

### 4.4. FSTEC Order No. 17 (K1 — state IS)
- Application of information security tools against unauthorised access, control over administrators' actions.
- Regular attestation of the IS.

### 4.5. FSTEC Order No. 21 (UZ-1 ISPDn)
- Compliance with the requirements for personal data protection.

### 4.6. GOST R 57580.1 (level 4)
- Implementation of all 8 areas of protection.
- Regular assessment of compliance under GOST R 57580.2 by an external auditor.

## 5. IS operating model

### 5.1. 24×7 SOC
- L1 — monitoring and primary classification.
- L2 — incident response, containment.
- L3 — investigation, root-cause analysis, attack termination.
- Threat hunting — proactive threat search.

### 5.2. Threat Intelligence
- Connection to BDU FSTEC.
- Subscription to NCCCI and Bank of Russia reports.
- Internal TI service (R-Vision TIP / a domestic analogue).

### 5.3. Vulnerability management
- Infrastructure scanning — weekly.
- Application scanning (DAST) — weekly.
- External pentest — annually by an accredited laboratory.
- Internal pentest — semi-annually by the Red Team.

### 5.4. Audit and compliance
- Annual internal IS audit.
- Annual external audit of compliance with GOST R 57580.1.
- Regular checks by regulators (CBR, FSTEC, FSB, Roskomnadzor).

## 6. Incident response plan

### 6.1. Incident categories
- **Category A (critical):** compromise of the issue circuit, of UKEP keys, mass leak of personal data.
- **Category B (high):** compromise of a single service, attempt at unauthorised DFA issue.
- **Category C (medium):** suspicious activity, single incidents.
- **Category D (low):** background events.

### 6.2. Response regulation
1. Detection → classification (5 min).
2. Notification of the SOC management (10 min).
3. Containment (15 min for critical).
4. Notification of the Bank of Russia and GosSOPKA (1 h for critical).
5. Investigation and remediation.
6. Post-mortem and lessons learned.

## 7. Personal data protection

### 7.1. Principles
- Privacy by Design.
- Minimisation of data collection.
- User consents are recorded in ESIA with the possibility of revocation.
- Right to access, correction, deletion (FZ-152).
- Transfer only to authorised bodies.

### 7.2. Localisation
- All personal data are stored and processed exclusively on the territory of the Russian Federation (FZ-152, Art. 18).
