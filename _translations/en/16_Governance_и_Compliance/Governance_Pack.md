## 1. Board of Directors (BoD)

### 1.1. Composition of the BoD

**Target structure: 5 BoD members, 3 of whom are independent (60% independence ratio).** Tier-1 PE / VC governance standard, required by LP investors and the Big-4 vendor DD.

| Seat | Category | Term | Appointed by |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chair of the BoD | Independent Director | 3 years | General Meeting of Shareholders |
| BoD member representing the Author (A.-Kh. A. Kagirov) | Executive Director | 3 years | Pre-emptive right |
| BoD member representing the Seed investor | External non-executive | 3 years | Shareholders Agreement |
| Independent Director #2 (Finance) | Independent | 3 years | Until Series A |
| Independent Director #3 (Tech / Cyber) | Independent | 3 years | Until Series A |

### 1.2. Powers of the BoD

- Approval of the strategy and the annual business plan.
- Approval of major transactions (> 5% of assets or > RUB 100M per year).
- Appointment of CEO, CFO, CTO, CISO.
- Approval of the Audit Plan and the external auditor (on the proposal of the Audit Committee).
- Approval of the risk appetite framework (on the proposal of the Risk Committee).
- Approval of the top-management compensation plan (on the proposal of the Compensation & Nomination Committee).
- Oversight of compliance with the law and regulatory requirements.

### 1.3. Meetings and quorum

- **Ordinary meetings:** no less than four times a year (quarterly).
- **Extraordinary meetings:** upon the request of any BoD member or of 10% of the shareholders.
- **Quorum:** ≥ 3 members; for critical decisions (M&A, equity issuance, amendment of the Articles of Association) — all 5 members are present.
- **Voting:** simple majority; for critical matters — qualified majority of 4 out of 5.

## 2. BoD Committees

### 2.1. Audit Committee

- **Composition:** 3 members, at least 2 independent, Chair independent.
- **Functions:**
  - Oversight of the reliability of financial reporting (IFRS + RAS).
  - Appointment of and interaction with the external auditor (Big-4 preferred).
  - Oversight of internal audit.
  - Oversight of the whistleblower system.
  - Approval of non-audit services from the external auditor (independence safeguard).
- **Meetings:** no less than four times a year.

### 2.2. Risk Committee

- **Composition:** 3 members, Chair independent.
- **Functions:**
  - Approval of the Risk Appetite Statement.
  - Oversight of management of:
    - Regulatory risk (changes in 779-P, 259-FZ, the tax regime)
    - Operational risk (incidents, BCP, DRP)
    - Cyber-security (SOC 2, ISO 27001, FSTEC CII-1)
    - Financial risk (cash runway, FX, IRR sensitivity)
    - Legal risk (litigation, IP)
  - Approval of the Recovery and Resolution Plan (the "living will").

### 2.3. Compensation & Nomination Committee

- **Composition:** 3 members, Chair independent.
- **Functions:**
  - Determination of the top-management compensation structure (cash + equity + LTI).
  - Approval of the ESOP (Employee Stock Option Plan).
  - Selection of BoD candidates (succession planning).
  - Annual performance review of the CEO / CFO / CTO / CISO.

### 2.4. IT & Cyber-Security Committee

- **Composition:** 3 members, at least 1 with a tech background, Chair an independent Tech Director.
- **Functions:**
  - Oversight of the tech roadmap.
  - Approval of major IT investments (> RUB 50M).
  - Oversight of compliance with FSTEC / SOC 2 / ISO 27001.
  - Oversight of cyber-security incidents (incident response plan).
  - Oversight of the engagement of external penetration testing.

## 3. Code of Conduct & Ethics

Based on the international standard (UN Global Compact + OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises 2023):

### 3.1. Principles

- Compliance with all applicable laws (Russian Federation + friendly jurisdictions).
- Protection of human rights (UN UDHR).
- Honest dealing with clients, partners and regulators.
- Personal-data protection (152-FZ + GDPR readiness).
- Protection of intellectual property.
- Corporate social responsibility.

### 3.2. Prohibited practices

- Bribery and corruption (UK Bribery Act + 273-FZ + FCPA readiness).
- Conflict of interest without full disclosure.
- Insider trading.
- Discrimination and harassment.
- Modern slavery / human trafficking (UK Modern Slavery Act 2015).

## 4. Anti-Bribery & Corruption Policy

- Compliance with Federal Law 273-FZ "On Counteracting Corruption".
- Compliance with the UK Bribery Act 2010 (for international LPs).
- Compliance with the FCPA 1977 (readiness; in case of US investors).
- Prohibitions:
  - "Facilitation payments" — of any kind.
  - Gifts and entertainment > RUB 5,000 for state officials without written approval.
  - Sponsorship of political parties.
- Third-party due diligence (KYC) on partners and counterparties.
- Mandatory annual anti-corruption training for all employees.
- Whistleblower mechanism with protection from retaliation.

## 5. ESG Policy

### 5.1. SASB Standards (Sustainability Accounting Standards Board)

Applicable standard: **Software & IT Services Industry (TC-SI) + Financial Services / Investment Banking & Brokerage (FN-IB)**.

| SASB category | Metric | Target value |
|---|---|---|
| Environmental Footprint of Hardware Infrastructure | Total energy consumed, % renewable | ≥ 30% renewable by 2028 |
| Data Privacy & Freedom of Expression | Number of data subjects affected by a data breach | 0 |
| Data Security | Number of incidents involving personally identifiable info | 0; SOC 2 Type II controls |
| Recruiting & Managing a Global Workforce | % gender / minority representation | ≥ 30% women in technical roles by 2028 |
| Intellectual Property Protection & Competitive Behavior | Legal proceedings amount | Disclosed quarterly |
| Managing Systemic Risks from Technology Disruptions | % uptime, business continuity | ≥ 99.95% uptime SLA |

### 5.2. TCFD Framework (Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures)

- **Governance:** ESG Committee at BoD level (under the Risk Committee).
- **Strategy:** analysis of physical and transitional climate risks for cloud infrastructure.
- **Risk Management:** integration of climate risks into Risk Appetite.
- **Metrics & Targets:**
  - Scope 1 emissions: tracked annually
  - Scope 2 emissions (cloud + offices): tracked annually
  - Renewable energy share for data centres: 30% by 2028, 70% by 2032
  - Net-zero alignment: 2040

## 7. Whistleblower Policy

- Independent reporting channel (external provider — Convercent / EthicsPoint / NAVEX Global).
- Anonymous reporting available 24/7.
- Protection from retaliation (dismissal, demotion, harassment).
- Investigation under the supervision of the Audit Committee.
- Annual reporting to the BoD.

## 8. Data Governance & Privacy

- **DPO (Data Protection Officer):** mandatory appointment (Article 22.1 of 152-FZ; Article 37 of the GDPR).
- **Data Inventory + Records of Processing Activities (RoPA):** kept current, updated quarterly.
- **Privacy by Design + Privacy by Default:** mandatory principles (Article 25 of the GDPR).
- **Data subject rights:** processing of requests within ≤ 30 days.
- **Data breach response plan:** notification to regulators within ≤ 24 h (Roskomnadzor) / ≤ 72 h (GDPR).
