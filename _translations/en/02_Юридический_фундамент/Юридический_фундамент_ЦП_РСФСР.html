Legal Foundation

Автор: Кагиров А.-Х.А. · Свидетельство о депонировании №4011265 от 19.12.2024 · Center Group Company · Engagement: CPFSR-RF-DESIGN-2026

Legal Foundation of the “Digital Platform RSFSR” Project

Russian Service for Financing of Social Development

Author and rights holder of the concept: Abdul-Khakim Akhmadovich Kagirov Deposit certificate: No. 4011265 of 19.12.2024 (ireg.pro), GOST-R 34.11-2012 hash. Purpose of the document: the legal frame of the platform-operator for issuance of digital financial assets against collateral. Stage 1 (pilot-light 2026–2028) — infrastructure funding via the Project Finance Factory of VEB.RF (Resolution of the Government of the Russian Federation No. 158 of 15.02.2018) and the Federal Treasury (Budget Code of the Russian Federation); the funds are directed to national projects within the prevailing legal field without amendments to Federal Law No. 86-FZ. Stage 2 (horizon 2029+) — a defensible expansion of the mandate towards a model of targeted secured emission by the Bank of Russia after a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months and adoption of the package of 4 FZ amendments.

1. Legal classification of the platform and its operator

1.1. Basic legal position

The Digital Platform RSFSR, by its legal nature, corresponds to the concept of an “information system within which digital financial assets are issued” in the meaning of Federal Law No. 259-FZ of 31.07.2020 “On Digital Financial Assets, Digital Currency, and on Amendments to Certain Legislative Acts of the Russian Federation” (hereinafter — Law No. 259-FZ).

The Platform Operator is classified as:

  • Operator of the information system within which DFAs are issued (clause 1 of art. 5 of Law No. 259-FZ). The powers and duties of the operator are set out in arts. 5–7 of the said Law.
  • At the second stage of the project’s development — also as operator of a digital financial assets exchange (art. 10 of Law No. 259-FZ), if the architecture implements functions of secondary DFA circulation among qualified participants.

Inclusion of the operator in the Bank of Russia register is carried out under the rules established by:

  • arts. 5 and 6 of Law No. 259-FZ;
  • Bank of Russia Regulation No. 779-P of 16.11.2021 “On the requirements upon meeting which a person intending to carry out the activity of an operator of an information system within which digital financial assets are issued is included by the Bank of Russia in the register of operators of information systems” (in the version in force);
  • Bank of Russia Regulation No. 780-P of 16.11.2021 “On the activity of an operator of a digital financial assets exchange and on the requirements for an operator of a digital financial assets exchange”.

1.2. Requirements as to the legal form of the operator

According to part 1 of art. 5 of Law No. 259-FZ, the operator of an information system may be:

  • a Russian legal entity in the form of a non-public joint-stock company or a limited liability company (for an operator that is a non-bank organisation);
  • a credit institution;
  • a trade organiser (art. 9 of Federal Law No. 325-FZ of 21.11.2011 “On Organised Trading”).

For the purposes of CP RSFSR, the model of a specialised joint-stock company with state or mixed capital is appropriate, which reflects the national significance of the project and simplifies interaction with the Bank of Russia and the Federal Treasury.

1.3. Minimum capital and infrastructure requirements

In accordance with the requirements of Bank of Russia Regulation No. 779-P, the operator must confirm:

  1. The amount of own funds — not less than the amount established by a normative act of the Bank of Russia as of the date of filing (a reserve of own funds of at least ₽50 million at the point of entry to the register and at least ₽500 million at the stage of industrial operation is assumed for CP RSFSR, which exceeds the minimum required).
  2. Existence of rights to the hardware-and-software complex of the information system.
  3. Existence of certified information-security means (FSTEC of Russia, FSB of Russia).
  4. Internal documents: rules of the information system (art. 6 of Law No. 259-FZ), internal control rules under 115-FZ, rules on the processing of personal data (152-FZ), information-security policy, operational-risk management regulation.
  5. Compliance with the business-reputation requirements applicable to the executive officers and controlling persons.

4. DFA-issuance financing: two-stage architecture

Principle: CP RSFSR operates in two sequential stages. Stage 1 (pilot-light 2026–2028) is launched within the existing legal field without amendments to Federal Law No. 86-FZ. Stage 2 (horizon 2029+) — expansion of the mandate only after a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months of industrial operation.

4.1. Stage 1 — pilot-light within the existing legal field (2026–2028)

Basic construction:

  1. The operator of the DFA-issuance information system (Atomyze or another subject of the 779-P register — the technological partner) issues DFAs in its information system, with backing by a pledged asset. The 779-P licence is personal and non-transferable; CP RSFSR acts as a front-end agent.
  2. DFA placement is financed via an infrastructural channel: the Project Finance Factory of VEB.RF (Government Resolution No. 158 of 15.02.2018) and/or the Federal Treasury within the powers of the Budget Code of the Russian Federation.
  3. Settlements for issuances — via the cashless rouble or the digital rouble under 340-FZ as a settlement complement (not as an issuance source).
  4. The Bank of Russia is the general supervisory regulator of the financial market under art. 4 of Federal Law No. 86-FZ; approves the operator’s rules under art. 6 of Federal Law No. 259-FZ; includes the operator in the register under 779-P. Additional emission by the Bank of Russia is not envisaged in Stage 1.

Applicable regulatory base for Stage 1: 259-FZ; 779-P / 780-P / 4336-U of the Bank of Russia; the Budget Code (budgetary procedures); Government Resolution No. 158 (VEB.RF Factory); arts. 334–358, 358.1–358.8, 421, 860.7–860.10, 926.1–926.8 of the CC; 340-FZ (the digital rouble as settlement infrastructure).

4.2. Stage 2 — horizon of mandate expansion (2029+, T+36+)

Entry condition for Stage 2: a positive Stage 1 track record of at least 12 months of industrial operation; a consolidated interagency position of the Ministry of Economic Development / Ministry of Finance / VEB.RF / State Duma.

Horizon hypothesis (a subject of further development, not the operating model of the near-term contour):

  • the institution of targeted secured emission by the Bank of Russia against a pool of pledge-backed DFAs with disclosure via a special escrow account managed by the Bank of Russia;
  • the category of “targeted infrastructure DFA” with regulation of the swap procedure;
  • integration with the digital-rouble infrastructure under 340-FZ.

Condition for the implementation of Stage 2: adoption of the package of 4 FZ amendments (86-FZ, 259-FZ, the Budget Code, the Civil Code) + by-laws. The full list is in section 8.2.

Marking for the regulator and investor: in current communication (concept, decks, business plan, DD report) the operating model is considered exclusively to be Stage 1. Stage 2 is presented as a defensible horizon whose implementation depends on the legislative work of the State Duma and on a decision of the Board of Directors of the Bank of Russia and is not part of the near-term investment roadmap.

5. Special escrow accounts managed by the Bank of Russia

5.1. Civil-law basis

The escrow-account institution is established by arts. 860.7–860.10 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation. Parties to the escrow-account contract: the account-holder, the beneficiary, the escrow agent (in CP RSFSR — the Bank of Russia).

The basic construction in the project:

  • Account-holder — the CP RSFSR operator as the person in whose favour the emission has been carried out.
  • Beneficiary — the Russian Federation represented by the Federal Treasury and the administrators of budgetary funds.
  • Escrow agent — the Bank of Russia.
  • Condition for releasing the account — the occurrence of smart-contract events (confirmation of the targeted spending on national projects, the act of commissioning the facility, the DFA swap).

5.2. The digital rouble as a settlement form

Since the Bank of Russia is the operator of the digital-rouble platform (clause 1 of Chapter X of Law No. 86-FZ as amended by No. 340-FZ), the targeted funds may be recorded in the form of special digital wallets of the Bank of Russia, which provides additional technological control over the movement of the funds.

5.3. Prohibition on free circulation of the funds

In accordance with item 10 of the author’s concept, the monetary funds are used at the Bank of Russia strictly in accordance with the federal laws and state acts in force for the realisation of the defined tasks. This prohibition shall be expressly entrenched at the level of:

  • the terms of the special escrow contract;
  • the Bank of Russia’s internal regulation;
  • a draft Resolution of the Government of the Russian Federation regulating the distribution of the targeted-emission funds.

6. The collateral encumbrance and the smart contract

6.1. Contractual construction

The civil-law basis of the relationship between the owner and the platform:

  • A mixed-type contract (art. 421 of the CC) — combines elements of pledge (Chapter 23 of the CC), of a services contract (Chapter 39 of the CC), of a DFA-issuance contract (arts. 4, 7 of Law No. 259-FZ), of a conditional-deposit contract (arts. 926.1–926.8 of the CC), of an insurance contract (Chapter 48 of the CC) and of an escrow-account contract (arts. 860.7–860.10 of the CC).
  • The contract is executed as a smart contract — a machine-executable software agreement whose legal force is established by item 1 of part 2 of art. 4 of Law No. 259-FZ and reinforced by arts. 309, 310 and 421 of the CC.

6.2. Registration of the encumbrance

For immovable property the encumbrance in the form of a pledge is registered in the Unified State Register of Real Estate in accordance with:

  • arts. 4, 8, 9, 14 of Law No. 218-FZ;
  • arts. 11, 19 of Law No. 102-FZ;
  • the Order of Rosreestr regulating electronic interaction.

For movable property — in the register of pledge-of-movable-property notifications of the Federal Notarial Chamber under the procedure established by art. 339.1 of the CC and the Fundamentals of Legislation on the Notariat.

For exclusive rights — state registration of the pledge with Rospatent (arts. 1232, 1365 of the CC).

For securities — entry of the encumbrance record by a depository (art. 7 of Law No. 39-FZ).

6.3. Material terms of the smart contract

Pursuant to item 4 of part 2 of art. 4 of Law No. 259-FZ and the developed regulatory practice, the material terms of the CP RSFSR smart contract are:

  1. identification of the owner of the asset and the operator;
  2. characteristics and appraised value of the pledged asset;
  3. amount and procedure of DFA issuance;
  4. period of validity of the DFA and conditions of return;
  5. procedure and amount of payment for the issuance and servicing of the DFA;
  6. amount and procedure of remuneration to the owner of the collateral;
  7. terms of risk insurance and the procedure for realising the insurance DFA;
  8. terms of the DFA swap upon commissioning of the new facility;
  9. procedure for signing the Mutual Settlements Act;
  10. procedure for removal of the encumbrance and return of the property to the owner.

7. DFA swap upon commissioning of the facility

7.1. Legal construction

The DFA swap is legally implemented as:

  • replacement of the subject matter of the pledge in the meaning of art. 345 of the CC (with the consent of the pledgee — the Bank of Russia);
  • simultaneous termination of the owner’s initial DFA and issuance of a new DFA on the built facility (arts. 4, 7 of Law No. 259-FZ);
  • automatic execution of the conditions of the smart contract (art. 309 of the CC).

A fundamental condition: the value of the new DFA always exceeds the value of the initial DFA, which covers the Bank of Russia’s costs of attracting and servicing the DFA and ensures the economic stability of the model (items 6 and 7 of the offer description in the original deck).

7.2. Tax consequences

  • For an individual owner: the operation of swapping the DFA for a fee-bearing encumbrance (including the receipt of remuneration) forms a taxable base for PIT in accordance with art. 214.11 of the Tax Code. A rate of 13% applies (15% for income above ₽5 mn per year under the progressive scale of the Tax Code).
  • For a legal-entity owner: the corresponding income is recognised within taxable profit (Chapter 25 of the Tax Code), with the expenses on the issuance and servicing of the DFA being recognised within expenses on a general basis.
  • VAT does not arise on operations of DFA issuance and circulation by virtue of sub-clause 12.2 of clause 2 of art. 149 of the Tax Code (introduced by Federal Law No. 324-FZ of 31.07.2020, in force in the current version from 01.01.2023). The application of sub-clauses 1 and 38 of clause 2 of art. 149 of the Tax Code to DFAs is incorrect: those sub-clauses regulate general financial services and equity instruments, whereas DFAs are singled out by the legislator into a separate category of relieved operations under sub-clause 12.2.

8. Regulatory base: the near contour (pilot-light) and the horizon (mandate expansion)

The structure of this section was changed in light of the consolidated clarifying audit of 24.05.2026. The previous version placed the package of 4 FZ amendments into the near-term roadmap, which contradicted the wedge strategy of “without 86-FZ amendments” and created an internal fault line in the package. The new version clearly separates the near contour (uses the legislation in force) and the T+24+ horizon (only considered after a positive wedge-pilot track record of ≥ 12 months).

8.1. Near contour (pilot-light 2026–2028): launch WITHOUT FZ amendments

The pilot-light regime operates within the legislation of the Russian Federation in force. No additional federal laws are required. The following are used:

  • Federal Law No. 259-FZ of 31.07.2020 “On Digital Financial Assets…” — DFA issuance within the operating perimeter under 779-P / CBR Directive 4336-U.
  • Government Resolution No. 158 of 15.02.2018 “On the Programme ‘Project Finance Factory’” — targeted infrastructure funding via VEB.RF.
  • The Budget Code of the Russian Federation (arts. 78, 78.1, 242.23) — targeted use of funds through the Federal Treasury.
  • Civil Code of the Russian Federation (arts. 860.7–860.10) — escrow account for targeted backing.
  • Federal Law No. 82-FZ of 17.05.2007 “On the Development Bank” (Vneshekonombank / VEB.RF).
  • Federal Law No. 223-FZ of 13.07.2015 “On Self-Regulatory Organisations in the Financial Market” (professional self-regulation).
  • Federal Law No. 224-FZ of 27.07.2010 “On Combating the Misuse of Insider Information…” (applicable to an operator-issuer).

Within this contour the project is already fully defensible — FZ amendments are not required to launch.

8.2. Horizon T+24+: package of 4 FZ amendments (mandate expansion)

Not the subject of the immediate roadmap. To be considered only after a positive wedge-pilot track record of ≥ 12 months of industrial operation. Goal — expansion of the platform’s mandate and the transition to the model of targeted secured emission by the Bank of Russia on the 2029+ horizon:

  1. Draft federal law “On Amendments to the Federal Law ‘On the Central Bank of the Russian Federation (Bank of Russia)’ regarding the regulation of targeted secured emission against a pool of digital financial assets”. Subject of regulation: introduction of the institute of targeted emission against secured DFAs; powers of the Bank of Russia as escrow agent; the regime of a special digital wallet for targeted emission; prohibition of off-platform circulation of DFAs.
  2. Draft federal law “On Amendments to the Federal Law ‘On Digital Financial Assets…’ regarding the introduction of the category of targeted infrastructure DFAs and regulation of the DFA-swap procedure”. Subject of regulation: the “targeted infrastructure DFA” category; circulation restrictions; the swap procedure; the special legal regime of insurance DFAs.
  3. Draft federal law “On Amendments to the Budget Code of the Russian Federation regarding the procedure for the receipt by the Federal Treasury of funds of targeted secured emission”.
  4. Draft federal law “On Amendments to the Civil Code of the Russian Federation” extending the escrow-account institute to special accounts of the Bank of Russia for placing the funds of targeted secured emission.

Filing under legislative initiative through a consolidated interagency position (Ministry of Economic Development → Ministry of Finance → Bank of Russia → State Duma). See the Champion map in the Concept and the Wedge document.

8.3. Level of by-laws (Stage 2 horizon)

  1. Resolution of the Government of the Russian Federation “On the Procedure for the Use of Targeted Secured Emission Funds for the Financing of National and Infrastructure Projects”.
  2. Bank of Russia Directive “On Licensing Requirements for the Operator of an Information System within which Targeted Infrastructure DFAs of the Digital Platform RSFSR Are Issued”.
  3. Bank of Russia Regulation “On Requirements for the Insurance of Digital Financial Assets in the System of Targeted Infrastructure Financing”.
  4. Joint Order of the Ministry of Finance of Russia / the Bank of Russia / the Ministry of Digital Development of Russia / Rosreestr / the Federal Treasury “On the Organisation of Information Interaction of the Digital Platform RSFSR with State Information Systems”.
  5. Order of FSTEC of Russia / FSB of Russia on Requirements for the Protection of Information in the Information System of CP RSFSR as a Critical Information Infrastructure Facility.
  6. Bank of Russia Standard on the Methodology of Appraisal of Pledged Assets for the Purposes of DFA Issuance on the CP RSFSR Platform.

  1. Rules of the CP RSFSR Information System (item 2 of art. 6 of Law No. 259-FZ) — the main public document of the platform.
  2. Internal control rules under 115-FZ.
  3. Personal data processing policy (152-FZ).

9. Licensing and procedure for inclusion in the Bank of Russia register

9.1. Stages

In accordance with art. 5 of Law No. 259-FZ and Bank of Russia Regulation No. 779-P, the operator undergoes the following stages:

  1. Preparation of the document package (IS rules, corporate documents, internal policies, description of the hardware-and-software complex, information-security documents).
  2. Filing of the application with the Bank of Russia, accompanied by the complete package.
  3. Consideration of the application within the prescribed period (typically not more than 30 business days from the submission of the complete package).
  4. Inclusion in the register of operators of information systems or a reasoned refusal.
  5. Entry of the information into the public register of the Bank of Russia.
  6. Continuous supervision under arts. 76.1–76.5 of Law No. 86-FZ.

9.2. Key checkpoints upon inclusion in the register

  • existence of sufficient own funds;
  • existence of certified information-security means;
  • compliance of the rules of the information system with the requirements of art. 6 of Law No. 259-FZ;
  • presence of persons satisfying the business-reputation requirements;
  • existence of an internal-control system and a risk-management system;
  • existence of an AML/CFT policy.

10. Identification and countering legalisation

10.1. KYC

Customer identification is carried out in accordance with:

  • art. 7 of Law No. 115-FZ;
  • Bank of Russia Regulation No. 499-P of 15.10.2015;
  • the use of the Unified Identification and Authentication System (UIAS; Government Resolution No. 584 of 10.07.2013);
  • the use of the Unified Biometric System (UBS; Bank of Russia Regulation No. 482-P).

Simplified identification is possible in cases expressly provided for by clause 1.4-2 of art. 7 of Law No. 115-FZ for DFA operations.

10.2. Monitoring of operations

The operator is required to:

  • perform mandatory control of operations falling under art. 6 of Law No. 115-FZ;
  • submit information to Rosfinmonitoring in the prescribed forms and timing;
  • apply a risk-based approach and record suspicious operations (art. 7 of Law No. 115-FZ).

10.3. DFA-specific features

Clause 1.4-2 of art. 7 of Law No. 115-FZ contains special identification requirements for DFA operations, which are implemented in full in the platform’s KYC module.

11. Personal data and critical information infrastructure

11.1. Personal data (152-FZ)

The CP RSFSR operator is a personal-data operator in the meaning of clause 2 of art. 3 of Law No. 152-FZ. Obligations:

  • existence of a personal-data processing policy (art. 18.1);
  • obtaining consents from data subjects;
  • ensuring personal-data protection requirements (art. 19);
  • registration in the Roskomnadzor register of operators;
  • compliance with a personal-data protection level not lower than UZ-1 in accordance with Government Resolution No. 1119 of 01.11.2012;
  • compliance with FSTEC Order No. 21 (requirements for PD protection in PDIS).

11.2. Critical information infrastructure (187-FZ)

The CP RSFSR information system falls under the definition of a CII facility:

  • sphere: “banking sphere and other spheres of the financial market” (art. 2 of Law No. 187-FZ);
  • significance category: the first (highest) — taking into account the scale of potential social, political and economic consequences of disruption.

The obligations of a CII subject (arts. 7, 9 of Law No. 187-FZ):

  • categorisation of CII facilities;
  • interaction with the State System of Detection, Prevention and Elimination of the Consequences of Computer Attacks (GosSOPKA);
  • compliance with the requirements of FSTEC of Russia (Order No. 239) and FSB of Russia;
  • notification of computer incidents.

12. The operator’s corporate structure

12.1. Minimum composition of internal documents

  1. Charter of the joint-stock company.
  2. Regulation on the Board of Directors and its committees (audit, risk, IT, remuneration).
  3. Regulation on the Management Board.
  4. Regulation on the Internal Audit Service.
  5. Regulation on the risk-management unit.
  6. Regulation on the internal control unit.
  7. Regulation on the AML/CFT unit (special compliance officer).
  8. Regulation on the information-security unit.
  9. Code of Business Ethics and Conflict-of-Interest Policy.

12.2. Officers subject to approval by the Bank of Russia

Within art. 14 of Law No. 259-FZ and Regulation No. 779-P, the following are subject to approval by the Bank of Russia:

  • the sole executive body (general director);
  • members of the collegial executive body;
  • the chief accountant;
  • the head of the internal-control service;
  • the head of the internal-audit service;
  • the special compliance officer on AML/CFT;
  • the head of the information-security unit.

13. Analysis of legal risks and barriers

No. Risk Severity Mitigation measures

14. Comparison with international practice

Jurisdiction Regulation of asset tokenisation Key differences from the CP RSFSR model
European Union MiCA Regulation (Markets in Crypto-Assets, Regulation (EU) 2023/1114) — in full force from 30.12.2024. MiCA regulates crypto-assets of a general nature; real-estate tokenisation remains within the national legal field of the member states; targeted central-bank financing against a pool of collateral is not provided for.
Switzerland The DLT Act (in force from 01.08.2021) — amendments to the Code of Obligations and the Financial Market Infrastructure Act. A strong legal infrastructure for DLT tokens as securities; the model does not imply direct emission of national currency against tokens.
Singapore Payment Services Act 2019 + the Singapore Asset Tokenisation regulatory regime (Project Guardian — MAS). Regulatory sandboxes for tokenisation, but without a full analogue of targeted emission against a collateral mass.
United Arab Emirates VARA (Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority, Dubai) and the ADGM regulation. Licensing of digital-asset operators; an RWA-tokenisation pilot, but without a link of emission to the national currency.
China E-CNY (digital yuan) — a two-tier model: CB–banks. Digital-currency emission by decision of the People’s Bank of China; pilots of asset tokenisation in technological zones; collateral backing is not enshrined by law.

The uniqueness of CP RSFSR in the international context consists in the two-stage wedge / pilot-light → mandate expansion architecture: Stage 1 is launched on infrastructure funding via the VEB.RF Factory (Government Resolution No. 158) + the Federal Treasury (Budget Code) without amendments to Federal Law No. 86-FZ; Stage 2 (horizon 2029+) — a defensible path towards the model of targeted secured emission against a pool of pledge-backed DFAs after a positive track record of the near contour. No analogous full-scale two-stage construction has been identified worldwide in the public domain at the time of preparation of this document.

15. Final conclusions

  1. Stage 1 (the near contour 2026–2028) is implementable within the current legal field without amendments to Federal Law No. 86-FZ. Base frame — Law No. 259-FZ; Bank of Russia Regulation No. 779-P / CBR Directive No. 4336-U (operator’s licence and own funds); Government Resolution No. 158 of 15.02.2018 (VEB.RF Project Finance Factory); the Budget Code of the Russian Federation (settlements through the Federal Treasury); the Civil Code of the Russian Federation (chapters on pledge and escrow). The wedge-pilot is launched through technological partnership with an existing operator of a DFA-issuance information system (Atomyze or another subject of the 779-P register).
  2. Stage 2 (horizon 2029+) — expansion of the mandate, not the subject of the near-term roadmap. Requires a package of 4 FZ amendments (86-FZ, 259-FZ, the Budget Code, the Civil Code) + by-laws (see sections 8.2 and 8.3). The entry condition is a positive Stage 1 track record of at least 12 months of industrial operation; an interagency position of the Ministry of Economic Development / Ministry of Finance / VEB.RF / State Duma.
  3. The role of the Bank of Russia in Stage 1 is the general supervisory regulator of the financial market under art. 4 of Federal Law No. 86-FZ: approval of the operator’s rules under art. 6 of Federal Law No. 259-FZ; inclusion of the operator in the register under 779-P; supervision of compliance with the information-system requirements (683-P, 716-P). Additional emission by the Bank of Russia in Stage 1 is not envisaged.
  4. Inflation-risk management in Stage 1 is ensured by the architecture of the infrastructural channel: VEB.RF Factory funding + the Budget Code is tied to projects with confirmed collateral backing appraised under Federal Law No. 135-FZ and does not create an unsecured expansion of the money supply.
