Due Diligence Report

Digital Platform CPFSR

Standard of work: Big Four-grade Due Diligence methodology (Deloitte / EY / KPMG / PwC). The document is prepared in the format of an internal DD report for the board of directors / investor. Object of the DD: the CPFSR project documentation as at 2025. Approach: review across six areas (Commercial / Legal / Financial / Technical / Operational / Tax & Regulatory) + a consolidated concluding section with honest red flags and recommendations.

1. Executive Summary

Structure of the DD

The report covers the six classic Due Diligence areas plus one additional (ESG / Sustainability), seven modules in total. Each module concludes with an assessment:

  • Green — no material observations;
  • Amber — observations to be addressed before closing;
  • Red — critical observations that must be remedied as a condition of the deal.

2. Commercial Due Diligence

2.1. Product analysis

CPFSR is a front-end / agent platform for the issuance of DFA against pledged assets, operating in a two-stage architecture. Stage 1 (pilot-light 2026–2028): placements are conducted through a technology partnership with an incumbent operator of the DFA information system under 779-P (Atomyze JSC or another register entity — the 779-P licence is individual and not transferable); funding flows through the VEB.RF Project Finance Factory (Government Decree No. 158) and the Federal Treasury under the Budget Code. Additional Bank of Russia issuance is not envisaged in Stage 1; the CBR is the supervisory regulator under Article 4 of Federal Law 86-FZ. Stage 2 (2029+ horizon): expansion of the mandate to a targeted secured-emission model against a pool of pledged DFA — a defensible horizon, implemented after a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months and adoption of the package of four federal laws. No analogues with such a two-stage architecture have been identified in public sources in the Russian Federation or in international markets.

2.2. Market analysis

  • Aggregate Russian real-estate value — approximately RUB 507 tn.
  • Conservative collateral capacity — RUB 25.5 tn.
  • Target market penetration by Year 10 — 20% (~RUB 5 tn), equivalent to ~3% of the annual nominal GDP of the Russian Federation.
  • No competitors with an analogous model exist today.

2.3. Competitive environment

  • The domestic DFA market is growing; current 779-P register operators include Sber, Alfa, Atomyze, Masterchain. In Stage 1 CPFSR operates as a front-end / agent through a technology partnership with one of them; there is no competition at the licensing level.
  • The differentiation of CPFSR is the wedge architecture: DFA placements are anchored to infrastructure funding through the VEB.RF Factory + the Federal Treasury, which turns the operator-partner into a distribution channel and CPFSR into a specialised infrastructure layer for targeted projects with collateral backing.
  • International references (Securitize, Polymath, HQLAx) — general-purpose asset tokenisation without targeted state financing.

2.4. Strategic partnerships

  • Implied work with the Bank of Russia, Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Digital Development, Federal Treasury, Rosfinmonitoring — all key state nodes.
  • A partner network of appraisers and insurers — potentially 100+ organisations.

  • Copyright belongs to A.-Kh. A. Kagirov — exclusively.
  • The deposit fixes the priority date. The legal protection regime — know-how (Article 1465 CC) and software (registration with Rospatent — in progress).

Assessment: 🟢 — the IP is duly formalised and protected.

3.2. Compliance with legislation

The platform relies on: - Federal Law 259-FZ (DFA); - Federal Law 86-FZ (on the CBR, including in the version of 340-FZ on the digital ruble); - Federal Law 161-FZ (National Payment System); - Federal Law 115-FZ (AML/CFT); - Federal Law 152-FZ (Personal Data); - Federal Law 187-FZ (CII); - Civil Code (pledge, escrow, smart contract); - CBR Regulations 779-P, 780-P, 683-P.

Assessment: 🟢 — the basic framework already exists in the current law.

3.3. Required regulatory actions

Stage 1 (2026–2028) — no amendments to federal laws. Launch of the near contour is possible within the current legal framework: - 259-FZ + CBR Regulation 779-P / Directive 4336-U — operator via technology partnership; - Government Decree No. 158 of 15.02.2018 — funding through the VEB.RF Project Finance Factory; - the Budget Code of the Russian Federation — settlements through the Federal Treasury; - the Civil Code — pledge and escrow.

Stage 2 (2029+ horizon) — a package of four federal laws + six sub-legislative acts (see the Legal Framework document, Sections 8.2 and 8.3). Entry condition — a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months and an inter-agency position of the Ministry of Economic Development / Ministry of Finance / VEB.RF / State Duma.

Assessment: 🟡 Amber — Stage 1 carries no binary regulatory risk and is delivered within the current framework; Stage 2 — a defensible horizon, non-blocking for the near-term investment roadmap.

  • Minimum capital — RUB 50M at entry, RUB 500M for the industrial phase.

Assessment: 🟡 — creating the operator legal entity is a task for the first 90 days after the round.

3.5. Licensing

Assessment: 🟡 — the process is clear but multi-stage, requires 6–9 months.

3.6. Agreements with state counterparties

At the DD date there are no formal agreements with the CBR, Ministry of Finance, or Federal Treasury — which is normal at the current (concept) stage.

Assessment: 🟡 — task for the preparation stage.

Legal DD result: 🟡 Amber

  3. Prepare the legislative drafts and make a presentation to the Bank of Russia within the first 180 days.

4. Financial Due Diligence

4.1. Financial model

A 10-year model, with BASE / CONSERVATIVE / OPTIMISTIC scenarios.

Key KPIs (BASE): - NPV @ WACC 29.5 = RUB 63.4 bn; - IRR ≈ 62% (with TV); - Payback = 2.8 years; - Peak financing need = RUB 1.3 bn (starter round); - Year-5 revenue = RUB 37 bn; - Year-10 revenue = RUB 138 bn; - TV EBITDA margin ≈ 74.8% (reflects the infrastructure nature, taking into account OpEx add-ons for compliance, cyber-security, legal support and a pool of appraisers/insurers).

Model assumptions: - Share of the RUB 25.5 tn market grows from 0.5% in Year 2 to 20% in Year 10. - Average DFA issuance commission: 0.5% of nominal. - Servicing commission: 0.3% per annum on outstanding DFA. - CapEx: RUB 850 mn over 18 months; OpEx: RUB 380 mn/year with +10%/year indexation. - WACC: 29.5 (based on 10Y OFZ + risk premium).

4.2. Sensitivity

  • WACC ±3 p.p. → NPV ±25%;
  • Market share ×0.5 → NPV –27%;
  • Commission ±20% → NPV ±14.5.

The sensitivity is asymmetric: even conservative scenarios (share ×0.5; WACC 25%) yield an NPV of ~RUB 57 bn — the model is resilient.

4.3. Revenue assumptions

  • The revenue mix evolves from primary placement commissions (Year 5: 39%) to a servicing margin on outstanding DFA (Year 10: 58%). This is typical for an infrastructure operator and means greater predictability of cash flows over the horizon.

Assessment: 🟢 Green

The model is rigorous, the assumptions reasonable, the sensitivity analysis is in place.

Recommendations

  1. Support the commission assumptions with benchmarks from Russian DFA operators (Sber, Atomyze).
  2. Prepare a quarterly model for the first 24 months for cash-flow management of the pilot phase.

5.2. Technology stack

Assessment: 🟢 — all components are realistic, in the Ministry of Digital Development register or FSTEC-certified.

5.3. DLT register

5.4. Security

  • GOST R 57580.1 level 4.
  • Personal-data protection level UZ-1.

Assessment: 🟡 — an ambitious but achievable profile. It requires a strong security team and significant certification spend.

5.5. Team

  • At the DD date the team is not formed (which is normal at the current stage).

Assessment: 🟡 — hiring 120 people in 12–18 months requires resourcing; the mitigation is a partnership with a large integrator (KROK / IBS / Digital Economy League / ICL).

Technical DD result: 🟡 Amber

7. Tax & Regulatory Due Diligence

7.1. Tax regime

DFA transactions are governed by: - Article 214.11 of the Tax Code (personal income tax); - Chapter 25 of the Tax Code (profit tax); - sub-paragraph 12.2 of paragraph 2 of Article 149 of the Tax Code (Federal Law of 31.07.2020 No. 324-FZ — exemption of DFA transactions from VAT) (partial VAT exemption).

Assessment: 🟡 — DFA tax practice is being formed; written clarifications from the Ministry of Finance / FNS are recommended at the pilot stage.

7.2. Regulatory fees

  • CBR: no licensing fees (separate state-duty payers).
  • Rosfinmonitoring: no special fees.

7.3. Tax burden under the model

  • Effective profit tax rate: 20% (standard).
  • Social contributions: 30%.
  • VAT: applicability across operations to be clarified.

Result: 🟡 Amber

Recommendations

  1. Obtain Ministry of Finance clarifications on the taxation of DFA exchange transactions.
  2. Obtain FNS clarifications on PIT accounting of operations.

8. ESG / Sustainability Due Diligence

8.1. Social effect

  • Development of national projects — a mass social effect.
  • Additional income for collateral owners — financial inclusion.
  • Increased employment in infrastructure projects.

8.2. Environmental footprint

  • The platform is digital; the principal environmental effect is data-centre energy consumption.
  • A permissioned DLT register — environmentally neutral, unlike PoW blockchains.

8.3. Corporate governance

  • A transparent structure; all operations are in an immutable audit log.
  • Regulatory oversight by the Bank of Russia.

Result: 🟢 Green

The ESG profile is strong; the project is an excellent case for state ESG funding.

9. Consolidated list of Red and Amber flags

Red flags (must be remedied as a condition of the deal)

At the current stage no critical red flags have been identified. The regulatory dependency (amendments to 86-FZ and 259-FZ) has been moved to Amber on the back of a mitigation plan.

Amber flags (a plan must be approved within the first 180 days of closing)

  1. The operator legal entity has not been created.
  2. Bank of Russia licence not yet obtained (planned receipt — 12–15 months).
  3. DLT provider not selected.
  4. The team is not formed (120 persons by the end of Year 2).
  5. No agreements with appraisal and insurance partners.
  6. DFA tax practice is not settled.
  7. Binary regulatory risk (legislative package).

Recommendations on deal structuring

  1. Tranching of investments linked to the achievement of milestones (creation of the legal entity, obtaining the licence, issuance of the first DFA).
  2. Reservation of 20% of the round for parallel legal support of the legislation (a critical task).
  3. Licence condition: the final tranche is tied to inclusion in the CBR register of IS DFA operators.
  4. Board of directors of the operator — with investor representatives and independent directors (a minimum of 30% independent).
  5. Employee option plan (ESOP) of 10% of the pool — a mandatory condition for retaining key staff.

10. Final conclusion

The project is recommended for starter financing subject to:

  1. Creation of the operator legal entity in the first 90 days.
  2. An approved plan for obtaining the CBR licence by Month 12.
  3. An approved plan for parallel work with legislators on the legislative package.
  4. Selection of a CEO and CTO in the first 60 days.
  5. A PoC of two DLT providers in the first 90 days.

Target starter round: USD 15M (RUB 1.335 bn at the rate of 71.55) at a post-money of USD 115 (RUB 8.455 bn) under the Base scenario (pre-money USD 100); investor stake — 13.04. Reasoned range of USD 70–140M post-money.

Main argument: the “need-to-potential” ratio = 1 : 20,000. Even the conservative scenario yields an NPV in the tens of billions of rubles. The regulatory risk is the only significant one and it is binary with a clear mitigation plan.
