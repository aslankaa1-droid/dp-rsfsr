3. Data protection

3.1. At-rest data protection

Data type Storage Protection
Access tokens, refresh tokens iOS Keychain / Android Keystore / Windows Credential Locker by OS means with biometric protection
Cryptographic keys Secure Enclave / TEE by device means
Biometric identifiers (but not vectors) Protected storage AES-256
UKEP certificates Protected storage by OS means
Business data cache IndexedDB / SQLite AES-256 with a key from the protected storage
Application settings LocalStorage not encrypted (non-sensitive)

3.2. In-transit data protection

  • TLS 1.3 with GOST certificates (KriptoPro TLS) for all HTTPS exchanges.
  • mTLS for critical operations (DFA issue, smart contract signing).
  • Certificate pinning on the root CA of the level of the RF financial infrastructure.
  • HSTS for the web mode (PWA).

3.3. Message-level encryption

For critical operations (DFA issue, signing), additional payload encryption is applied at the application level: - Algorithm: GOST R 34.12-2015 (“Kuznyechik” / “Magma”). - Mode: GOST R 34.13-2015.

3.4. Data signing

  • User’s UKEP: GOST R 34.10-2012 with a key length of 256/512 bits.
  • Signature by hash function: GOST R 34.11-2012 (Streebog).
  • UKEP certificates are issued by certification authorities accredited by the Ministry of Digital Development of Russia.

4. Authentication and authorisation

4.1. Methods of authentication

Primary (entry into the application)

  • ESIA — OpenID Connect 1.0 with a confirmed account.
  • EBS — biometric identification (Regulation No. 482-P).

Local (repeat sign-in)

  • Device biometrics — Face ID / Touch ID / Windows Hello.
  • PIN code — 4–6 digits, protected storage.

Critical operations

  • Two-factor authentication: SMS code + biometrics.
  • UKEP signing.

4.2. Session management

  • Access token lifetime: 15 minutes.
  • Refresh token lifetime: 30 days.
  • On prolonged inactivity (10 minutes) — repeat biometric authentication.
  • On a change of device — all previous sessions are invalidated.

5. Application protection

5.1. Protection against device compromise

  • Jailbreak/Root detection — upon detection of compromise, the application:
    1. Displays a warning to the user.
    2. Blocks the DFA issue and signing functions.
    3. Preserves the read-only mode for existing DFAs.
    4. Sends the incident to the SOC.
  • Emulator detection — refusal to operate in emulators (for the production build).

5.2. Protection against reverse engineering

  • Code obfuscation for all platforms:
    • ProGuard / R8 for Android;
    • bitcode + Swift obfuscation for iOS;
    • .NET obfuscation for Windows;
    • JavaScript obfuscation for PWA.
  • Native code integrity — checksum verification on launch.

5.3. Protection against MITM attacks

  • Certificate pinning at two levels: root CA and intermediate.
  • Refusal to operate on suspicion of a custom CA installed on the device.

5.4. Screenshot protection

  • Screenshot protection enabled for the screens:
    • Smart contract signing.
    • OTP code.
    • Payout details.
    • Asset cards with the full address.

5.5. Runtime protection

  • Anti-debug — detection of debuggers and frida servers.
  • Anti-hook — integrity check of key functions.
  • Memory protection — clearing of secrets from memory after use.

6. Personal data protection (FZ-152)

6.1. Categories of personal data

The following categories of personal data are processed (in line with Article 3 of FZ-152): - Other categories — the main (full name, passport, INN, contacts, address). - Biometric — when working with EBS.

6.2. Consents

  • Consent to the processing of personal data — issued via ESIA on the first sign-in.
  • Possibility to revoke consent — via the “Profile → Security → Delete account” section.

6.3. Localisation

All personal data are processed and stored exclusively on the territory of the Russian Federation in line with item 5 of Article 18 of FZ-152.

6.4. Protection level

  • UZ-1 under RF Government Resolution No. 1119.
  • Compliance with FSTEC of Russia Order No. 21.

6.5. Notification of Roskomnadzor

The operator has been entered in the Roskomnadzor register of personal data operators.

7. Incident response

7.1. Incident categories

Category Description Response time Notification
Critical Compromise of UKEP keys, mass leak of personal data ≤ 1 hour Bank of Russia, GosSOPKA, NCCCI
High Compromise of a single account, attempt at unauthorised DFA issue ≤ 4 hours Bank of Russia
Medium Suspicious activity ≤ 24 hours Internal SOC
Low Background events On schedule Internal SOC

7.2. Notification procedure

In line with Bank of Russia Instruction No. 6635-U: - Critical incidents — within 1 hour. - High — within 24 hours. - Full report — within 30 days.

Notifications via the secured ViPNet channel in XML format.

8. Audit and monitoring

8.1. Journalising

All key application events are logged: - Authorisation (success/failure). - Asset submission. - Triggering of DFA issue. - Smart contract signing. - Receipt of payments. - Profile and settings changes. - Biometric data changes. - Security events (jailbreak, MITM, failed authentication).

8.2. Log storage

  • Operational logs: 13 months.
  • Security audit logs: 10 years.
  • Stored on the server-side, not on the device.

8.3. SIEM integration

All security events are sent to the operator’s SIEM system (KOMRAD / MaxPatrol SIEM / R-Vision SIEM) in real time.

9. Certification

9.1. FSTEC of Russia certificates

  • Compliance with Order No. 239 (CII of category 1).
  • Compliance with Order No. 17 (K1).
  • Compliance with Order No. 21 (UZ-1 personal data).

9.2. FSB of Russia certificates

  • Certification of cryptographic means.

9.3. Bank of Russia certificates

  • Compliance with Regulation No. 779-P (IS operator).
  • Compliance with Regulation No. 683-P.
  • Compliance with GOST R 57580.1 level 4.

9.4. Ministry of Digital Development of Russia register

  • Inclusion in the Unified Register of Russian Software (RF Government Resolution No. 1236).

9.5. Roskomnadzor registration

  • Notification of personal data processing.

10. Compliance control

  • Internal IS audit — annually.
  • External pentest by an accredited laboratory — annually.
  • Internal Red Team — every six months.
  • Compliance with GOST R 57580.1 verification — annually.
  • Regulatory checks — at the request of the CBR, FSTEC, FSB, Roskomnadzor.

The document describes the information security requirements for the CPFSR mobile application and is subject to refinement based on the results of certification testing.
