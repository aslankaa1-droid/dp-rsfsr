# Briefing Paper
## Digital Platform "Russian Service for Social Development Financing" (CPFSR)
### Off-budget financing of national and infrastructure projects on the basis of digital financial assets backed by the collateral capacity of citizens and organisations of the Russian Federation

## 1. Executive Summary

> **Architectural note (post-audit 24.05.2026).** The briefing paper describes the two-stage implementation of the platform:
>
> **Stage 1 (pilot-light 2026–2028)** — launch WITHOUT amendments to Federal Law 86-FZ. CPFSR operates as an IS DFA operator under existing 259-FZ in technology partnership with a licensed operator (including Atomyze JSC). Targeted funding is provided through the VEB.RF Project Finance Factory (Government Decree of the Russian Federation No. 158 of 15.02.2018) and the Federal Treasury within the existing Budget Code. The Bank of Russia is the general supervisory regulator of the financial market under Article 4 of 86-FZ.
>
> **Stage 2 (2029+ horizon)** — expansion of the platform's mandate and transition to the targeted secured-emission model of the Bank of Russia. The description below relates to this stage and is presented as a **horizon vision**. The package of four federal-law amendments (§ 5 below) is NOT part of the immediate roadmap. It is considered **only after a positive wedge-pilot track record of ≥ 12 months of industrial operation**.

The Digital Platform CPFSR is an operating infrastructure that ensures the issuance of digital financial assets (DFA) against the pledge of real estate and other liquid property of citizens and organisations of the Russian Federation.

**Stage 1 / pilot-light:** against the pool of pledge-backed DFA in 2026–2028, targeted funding is organised through the VEB.RF Project Finance Factory and the Federal Treasury on a dedicated escrow account with targeted control.

**Stage 2 / 2029+ horizon (defensible mandate expansion, not part of the immediate roadmap):** implemented after a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months of industrial operation and upon adoption of the package of four federal-law amendments. At this stage, against the aggregate portfolio of pledge-backed DFA, additional secured issuance by the Bank of Russia may be considered (in the longer perspective — in the form of the digital ruble), targetedly accumulated on dedicated escrow accounts under the Bank of Russia's management and channelled through the Federal Treasury for the financing of national and infrastructure projects. Upon completion of the projects, an exchange of DFA takes place: the owner's initial collateral is replaced by DFA on the newly built state object, the encumbrance is removed from the original asset, and the owner receives the agreed remuneration.

The platform addresses six strategic objectives:
- activates "dormant" private assets as a source of off-budget financing of state programmes;
- preserves the controlled-inflation regime, since the financing is performed strictly against secured and appraised assets (in Stage 1 — through the infrastructure channel of VEB.RF + the Federal Treasury; in Stage 2 — potentially through secured issuance by the CBR);
- reduces the queue of national-project financing;
- accelerates the development of the DFA industry in the Russian Federation in line with Federal Law No. 259-FZ of 31.07.2020;
- provides additional guaranteed income to owners of pledged property;
- strengthens the sovereignty of the financial system by replacing external debt financing with domestic funding.

The implementation period for the operator platform, according to the author's concept, is **from 18 months**.

## 2. Problem statement

The current stage of implementation of national projects and state programmes of the Russian Federation faces the simultaneous impact of the following constraints:

1. **Budget constraints.** Federal budget spending on investment programmes is limited by the requirements of the budget rule and by the priorities of social-block expenditures.
2. **High cost of debt funding.** Against the backdrop of the Bank of Russia key rate in recent years, raising commercial credit funding for infrastructure projects is becoming economically unjustified for the majority of state customers.
3. **Restricted access to international capital markets.** Under sanctions, traditional sources of external funding are closed or significantly more expensive.
4. **Delay in the full-scale launch of the digital ruble.** There is no mass practical basis for applying the digital ruble in large targeted programmes.
5. **Low monetisation of private assets.** The aggregate value of real estate in the Russian Federation is estimated at the order of RUB 507 tn, while the overwhelming part is not involved in the financial turnover.

These constraints form the **paradox of "a wealthy country with an investment money-mass deficit"**: in the presence of a vast stock of national wealth, its conversion into a source of infrastructure development remains disproportionately low.

## 3. Conceptual solution

> **Architectural principle.** CPFSR operates in two sequential stages. Stage 1 (pilot-light 2026–2028) is launched within the existing legal framework without amendments to Federal Law 86-FZ. Stage 2 (2029+ horizon) — expansion of the mandate with transition to the targeted secured-emission model of the Bank of Russia — is implemented only after a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months of industrial operation and adoption of the package of four federal-law amendments. Section 3.1 below describes the Stage 1 operating model; § 3.2 — the defensible Stage 2 horizon.

### 3.1. Principles of platform operation — Stage 1 (2026–2028)

1. The property owner (an individual, a legal entity, a state enterprise, an individual entrepreneur, a non-profit organisation, a fund) registers on the platform and undergoes identification through the Unified Identification and Authentication System (ESIA) and/or the Unified Biometric System (572-FZ).
2. The property object (real estate, transport, intellectual property, securities) is appraised by an authorised appraisal and/or insurance organisation under Federal Law 135-FZ and by the methodologies established by the state.
3. A temporary encumbrance is placed on the object in favour of the platform operator (Articles 334–358 of the Civil Code, Federal Law 102-FZ); the property remains in the disposal of the owner.
4. CPFSR, as a front-end / agent, agrees the issuance parameters with the technology partner — an incumbent operator of the DFA-issuance information system under 779-P (Atomyze JSC or another register entity — the 779-P licence is individual and is not transferable; the operator's rules are approved by the Bank of Russia under Article 6 of Federal Law 259-FZ).
5. The technology partner issues the DFA on the terms of a smart contract (term, size, exchange conditions, insurance, force majeure, owner remuneration, reconciliation act, the procedure for the removal of the encumbrance). The parties to the smart contract are the Pledgor, the Operator and the DFA acquirers.
6. The placement of the DFA is funded through the infrastructure channel: the VEB.RF Project Finance Factory (Government Decree of 15.02.2018 No. 158) and the Federal Treasury within the powers granted by the Budget Code of the Russian Federation. Settlements — in non-cash ruble or in the digital ruble under 340-FZ as a settlement complement. **No additional issuance by the Bank of Russia is envisaged in Stage 1.**
7. The funds are directed to state customers — specific ministries and subordinate organisations — for the implementation of approved national projects and federal programmes in the procedure established by the Budget Code and the sub-legislative acts.
8. Upon completion of the project, the new state-property object is appraised. A new DFA is issued against it, with a value exceeding the owner's initial DFA. An **exchange of DFA** takes place between the parties in accordance with the operator's rules under Article 6 of Federal Law 259-FZ: the owner's initial asset is released from the encumbrance, and the DFA on the built object enters circulation.
9. The initial encumbrance is removed. The owner receives the agreed remuneration. Voluntary prolongation and reinvestment of the collateral into new projects is possible.
10. Upon an insured event, the partner insurance organisation issues its own DFA through the platform to exchange for and compensate the owner's initial DFA (Law of the RF No. 4015-1 + Chapter 48 of the Civil Code).

### 3.2. Stage 2 horizon (2029+, T+36+) — defensible mandate expansion

Following a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months of industrial operation and in coordination among the Ministry of Economic Development / Ministry of Finance / VEB.RF / State Duma / Bank of Russia, a transition may be considered to the model of **targeted secured emission by the Bank of Russia against a pool of pledge-backed DFA**, implemented through a dedicated escrow account under the Bank of Russia's management with distribution through the Federal Treasury. Stage 2 requires the package of four federal-law amendments (86-FZ, 259-FZ, the Budget Code, the Civil Code) and the sub-legislative acts — see § 6. **Stage 2 is not part of the near-term investment roadmap and is not the subject of the current communication with the investor; it is presented as a defensible horizon, the implementation of which depends on the legislative work of the State Duma and the decision of the Board of Directors of the Bank of Russia.**

### 3.3. Key feature of Stage 1: inflation control

The infrastructure funding through the VEB.RF Factory and the Federal Treasury is performed **strictly against a secured and appraised asset** within budget procedures and **exclusively for the purposes of approved state programmes**. This eliminates the classic inflation risk of unsecured issuance. In Stage 1 the platform converts the "dormant" private capitalisation into active investment form without an increase in the money supply on the issuer's side.

### 3.4. Key Stage 1 principle — non-public DFA circulation

The DFA issued within CPFSR are not intended for free public circulation: their circulation is limited to professional securities-market participants and qualified acquirers in accordance with the operator's rules under Article 6 of Federal Law 259-FZ. This reduces the risk of manipulation and speculative pressure.

## 4. Collateral capacity

| Property segment | Total volume, RUB tn | Conservative estimate of the engaged mass (10% of assets × 50% of value), RUB tn |
|---|---:|---:|
| Residential stock (4.7 bn m² × avg. RUB 85,000/m²) | 400 | 20.0 |
| Commercial real estate (offices, retail, warehouses, hotels, industrial, other) | 45 | 2.5 |
| Land plots (housing, dachas, land under enterprises and infrastructure) | 62 | 3.0 |
| **TOTAL** | **507** | **25.5** |

Source: the author's concept and the calculation of the collateral-value potential of assets of the Russian Federation (excluding state-owned infrastructure facilities and movable property).

Thus, by phased involvement of even 5–10% of this conservative potential, the Russian Federation gains **up to RUB 1.3–2.5 tn of off-budget investment funding** at the pilot-contour horizon and **up to RUB 25 tn** at the full capacity of the industrial phase. This is comparable to the aggregate funding of national projects over a three-year horizon.

## 5. Effect for participants

### For the state
- Off-budget financing of national and social projects without an increase in the debt burden.
- Reduction of the queue of global-project financing.
- Economic growth through accelerated implementation of infrastructure projects and the associated multiplier (expert estimates put the coefficient at 1.8–2.3 per ruble of infrastructure investment).
- Development of the digital economy as one of the priorities of national development.
- A higher level of state social responsibility through accelerated fulfilment of social obligations.

### For the Bank of Russia
- Development of the Russian Federation's financial market in the format of a sovereign digital infrastructure.
- Steady increase in the money supply proportionate to the growth of the real economy and secured assets.
- Introduction and development of new payment instruments.
- Launch of the digital ruble within a large targeted contour — state national projects.
- A substantial contribution to the country's digitalisation.
- Full control over construction spending and national-project execution through smart contracts and escrow accounts.

### For the population and business
- Additional guaranteed income from one's own assets without alienating the property.
- Growth of employment in infrastructure projects (significant in the context of the return of the active population from the special military operation).
- Growth of population digital literacy.
- Higher quality of life through accelerated implementation of social projects.
- Ability to insure one's own real estate without additional spending during the period of its pledge.

- GOST R 57580.1 (information-protection requirements for financial organisations; target level — 4).

### Stage 1 (2026–2028) — no regulatory changes required

The near contour is launched on the current base: 259-FZ + Bank of Russia Regulations 779-P / 780-P + CBR Directive No. 4336-U; Government Decree No. 158 of 15.02.2018 (VEB.RF Project Finance Factory); the Budget Code (Federal Treasury); the Civil Code (pledge and escrow). Adjustments are made at the level of the operator's rules under Article 6 of Federal Law 259-FZ (approved by the Bank of Russia) and the contractual structure with the technology partner.

### Stage 2 (2029+ horizon) — package of four federal-law amendments and sub-legislative acts

**Not part of the near-term roadmap.** Implemented after a positive Stage 1 track record of ≥ 12 months and an inter-agency position of the Ministry of Economic Development / Ministry of Finance / VEB.RF / State Duma. Composition of the package:

1. **Amendments to Federal Law 86-FZ** legalising targeted secured issuance of monetary funds (including the digital ruble) against a pool of pledge-backed DFA, with mandatory targeted placement on dedicated escrow accounts.
2. **Amendments to Federal Law 259-FZ** introducing the category of "targeted infrastructure DFA" and regulating the procedure for DFA exchange.
3. **Amendments to the Budget Code** — the procedure for the Federal Treasury to receive the proceeds of targeted secured emission and to distribute them to state customers.
4. **Amendments to the Civil Code** — extension of the escrow-account institute to dedicated Bank-of-Russia accounts for the placement of targeted secured emission proceeds.
5. **A Government decree** on the procedure for the use of the targeted secured emission proceeds for the financing of national and infrastructure projects.
6. **A Bank of Russia Directive** on a special licensing regime for the operator of targeted infrastructure DFA.
7. **A Bank of Russia Regulation** on the insurance of DFA in the system of targeted infrastructure financing.
8. **A joint order of the Ministry of Finance of Russia / Bank of Russia / Ministry of Digital Development / Rosreestr / Federal Treasury** on the integration of the platform with Gosuslugi, the State Real Estate Register, SMEV, the State Information System of State and Municipal Payments and the Federal Treasury.

## 7. Technology contour

The platform is built exclusively on domestic components from the register of Russian software of the Ministry of Digital Development in accordance with Government Decree No. 1236 of 16.11.2015 and the import-substitution requirements of Presidential Decree No. 166 of 30.03.2022.

Key principles:
- Multiply fault-tolerant geo-distributed infrastructure (at least three data centres).
- Compliance with the 4th (maximum) level of protection under GOST R 57580.1.
- Categorisation of the information system as a critical-information-infrastructure object in accordance with Federal Law 187-FZ.
- Cryptographic means meeting GOST R 34.10-2012, GOST R 34.11-2012, GOST R 34.12-2015, GOST R 34.13-2015.
- A distributed register (DLT) for DFA accounting on a domestic platform certified by the Bank of Russia and the Ministry of Digital Development.
- Full integration with state information systems via SMEV 3.

(A detailed description of the architecture is provided in the project's Technical documentation.)

## 10. Role of the Bank of Russia

### 10.1. Stage 1 (2026–2028)

Within the near contour, the Bank of Russia acts as the **general supervisory regulator of the financial market** under Article 4 of Federal Law 86-FZ:
- maintains the register of operators of information systems in which DFA are issued (Regulation No. 779-P);
- approves the operator's information-system rules under Article 6 of Federal Law 259-FZ;
- controls compliance with the equity requirements for the operator under Directive No. 4336-U;
- supervises compliance with information-protection requirements (Regulations Nos. 683-P and 716-P) and GOST R 57580.1;
- exercises 115-FZ supervision jointly with Rosfinmonitoring.

**No additional issuance by the Bank of Russia is envisaged in Stage 1.** Issuance settlements run in non-cash ruble or in the digital ruble under 340-FZ as a settlement complement.

### 10.2. Stage 2 (2029+ horizon) — defensible mandate expansion

Within the 2029+ horizon and following adoption of the package of four federal-law amendments, the Bank of Russia may act as the **central trust node** in the system of targeted secured issuance:
- maintains the register of operators of targeted infrastructure DFA;
- manages the dedicated escrow accounts of the targeted secured issuance (following the extension of the escrow-account institute in the Civil Code);
- approves the methodology for the appraisal and insurance of collateral assets;
- performs targeted secured issuance strictly against a pool of secured and appraised DFA in the manner provided for by the amendments to 86-FZ;
- participates in the inter-agency committee on smart-contract control.

Stage 2 is presented as a defensible horizon, the implementation of which depends on the legislative work of the State Duma and the decision of the Board of Directors of the Bank of Russia. **Stage 2 is not part of the current communication with the investor and is not included in the near-term investment roadmap.**

The platform does not substitute for the functions of the Bank of Russia and the Federal Treasury but **serves** them through the technological and operational infrastructure.
