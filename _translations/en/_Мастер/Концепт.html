1. Conceptual essence (the author’s twelve points)

CPFSR is a digital platform-operator connected to government and banking services, ensuring the engagement of a collateral base for the issuance and servicing of Digital Financial Assets (DFA).

  1. The application connects to government and banking services.
  2. It ensures the engagement of a collateral base for the issuance and servicing of DFA.
  3. The collateral base includes real estate, transport, intellectual property, securities, and similar assets.
  4. Collateral is appraised automatically on the platform pursuant to enacted government regulations.
  5. The pledged property remains in the disposal of the owner, but a temporary encumbrance is imposed on the collateral object.
  6. Against the pledged property the platform operator issues DFA. In the pilot-light regime of 2026–2028 the management and placement of DFA take place within the perimeter of a licensed DFA Information System (IS) operator under Bank of Russia Regulation 779-P (technology partner). Beyond 2029, subject to the adoption of the legislative package, an expansion of the mandate is possible.
  7. DFA are issued by a single platform-operator that obtains (a) the status of an IS DFA operator in the Bank of Russia register pursuant to 779-P / Directive 4336-U, and (b) the accompanying regulatory clearances (Roskomnadzor, FSTEC, Rosfinmonitoring). The IS DFA operator licence is not transferable between legal entities; consequently, the pilot-light uses a technology-partnership construction with an incumbent operator.
  8. DFA are issued with the consent of the owner for the term required for the implementation of national, infrastructure projects and other state programmes.
  9. For each issued DFA, targeted funding is provided through the institutional infrastructure of VEB.RF / the Federal Treasury (in the pilot-light regime 2026–2028 — without amendments to Federal Law 86-FZ). Funds are credited to a dedicated escrow account under the supervision of an authorised financial institution. Beyond 2029 — subject to regulatory support and the adoption of the legislative package — a transition to the model of targeted secured emission by the Bank of Russia is possible (see Section 9-Horizon below).
  10. Funds are used strictly in accordance with current legislation (Federal Law 259-FZ, Federal Law 39-FZ, the Budget Code of the Russian Federation) and/or newly adopted federal laws and government regulations.
  11. DFA may be placed with qualified securities-market participants via a licensed IS DFA operator; proceeds from placement are credited solely to dedicated escrow accounts under banking supervision.
  12. Upon expiry of the DFA term, the pledged property is returned to the owner or reinvested by the owner in analogous projects.

1-bis. Wedge-pilot 2026–2028 and Horizon 2029+ (two-circuit strategy)

Pilot-light 2026–2028 (current circuit, no amendments to 86-FZ). CPFSR is launched as an IS DFA operator under the existing 259-FZ framework, in technology partnership with an incumbent operator (Atomyze JSC — as transaction operator under its 779-P licence; CPFSR — as front-end / collateral-onboarding agent). Targeted funding flows through the institutional infrastructure of VEB.RF (Project Finance Factory, Government Decree No. 158 of 15.02.2018) and the Federal Treasury (Budget Code). The Bank of Russia plays a supporting role as the supervisory regulator (Article 4 of 86-FZ), without authority to approve the targeted purpose of the credit.

Horizon 2029+ (targeted secured-emission model). Only following a positive track record of the wedge-pilot (≥ 12 months of industrial operation, demonstrated convergence of metrics on intellectual-property origination, repayment, and collateral realisation), the legislative package of four amendments (changes to 86-FZ, 259-FZ, the Budget Code, the Civil Code) described in § 8 of the Legal Framework moves from “horizon T+24+” to the active roadmap. This represents the author’s horizon-vision rather than the substance of the near-term communication with the regulator.

1-quater. Author protection (rightsholder of the know-how)

Principle: the author Abdul-Khakim Akhmadovich Kagirov is the sole rightsholder of the know-how under Certificate No. 4011265 of 19.12.2024. Following the contribution of the know-how to the charter capital of the operator JSC, the following protective mechanisms are fixed:

  1. Permanent seat on the Board of Directors until Series B (≥ 2031), independent of the size of the investor’s stake.
  2. Protective covenants in the Shareholders Agreement:
    • drag-along — only with the rightsholder’s participation;
    • tag-along — 100% protection from forced exit for the rightsholder;
    • anti-dilution — full ratchet for the rightsholder until Series B;
    • prohibition on assignment of know-how rights without the rightsholder’s consent (Article 1465 CC + licence agreement);
    • vesting of the institutional shareholder — 4 years with a 1-year cliff.
  3. Reverter of know-how upon liquidation / bankruptcy / hostile takeover: exclusive rights to the know-how revert to the author unconditionally.
  4. Royalty stream — 2% of the operator’s revenue payable to the rightsholder as licensor (alongside share dividends).

The above items are recorded in the founding agreement of the JSC and in the licence agreement between the rightsholder and the operator; they are written into the Shareholders Agreement at the first investment round.

2. Advantages (per the author’s source document)

  1. Direct online interaction between the pledgor, financial agents and specialised executors under the oversight of the Bank of Russia.
  2. Reduction in the time required for appraisal and pledging of objects.
  3. An impulse for the development of the DFA industry in Russia.
  4. Mobilisation of an enormous monetary mass through “dormant” private assets.
  5. Resolution of large-scale state projects.
  6. Reduction of the queue for financing global projects.
  7. Impetus to the real sector of the economy.
  8. Off-budget financing of state projects in capital-intensive industries (heavy industry, machine-building, medicine, science).
  9. Financial remuneration for the owner of the pledged property.
  10. Inflationary dynamics remain within the normal regime.

3. Disadvantages and conditions (per the author’s source document)

  1. Amendments to the legislation of the Russian Federation are required (new federal laws).
  2. A powerful political will is required.
